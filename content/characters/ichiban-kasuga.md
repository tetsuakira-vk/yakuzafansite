## Background

Ichiban Kasuga was born in a soapland in Yokohama's Ijincho district — literally in the toilet, on New Year's Day. His mother left him there. He was raised by the owner of the soapland, who treated him like family, but the circumstances of his birth were never far from his sense of himself: a person whose story started wrong.

He grew up obsessed with *Dragon Quest*, the JRPG series, internalising its framework — small person, overwhelming evil, unlikely allies, and the certainty that a hero never gives up — as his actual worldview. This is not played as naivety. The game takes his conviction seriously.

---

## Character

Ichiban is the anti-Kiryu in almost every structural way: loud, expressive, terrible at stoicism, constantly emoting, bad at tactics, heroically uncool. Where Kiryu's strength is immovability, Ichiban's is momentum — he will get knocked down, process it loudly, and get back up.

What he shares with Kiryu is the core: he doesn't abandon people. He can't. It's not a strategy or a code; it's just how he's built.

His Dragon Quest brain is not just a personality quirk — it is the source of his actual strength. He frames everything as a JRPG because that framework lets him find meaning in suffering, allies in strangers, and villains who can be defeated. It turns out this is not a bad way to live.

---

## Combat

In *Like a Dragon* and *Infinite Wealth*, Ichiban's combat visualises as JRPG battles: enemies and party members take turns, skills are cast as magical attacks, enemies are defeated with dramatic finishing moves. The in-universe explanation is that Ichiban's mind works this way — he literally perceives his brawls as Dragon Quest fights.

His primary job class is **Hero** (naturally), with abilities focused on rallying allies and breaking enemy will. His signature move, **Dragon Quest**, is a finisher that channels his genuine belief in the party.

---

## Key Story Beats

- **Like a Dragon**: Released from prison to find his patriarch has shot him and left him for dead. Survives. Builds a new family out of strangers in Ijincho. Uncovers the conspiracy at Ijincho's heart and confronts the man who abandoned him.
- **Infinite Wealth**: Travels to Hawaii to find his mother. Meets Kiryu. The story becomes a dual meditation on fathers, sons, and legacy.

---

## Why He Works

Ichiban works because he is genuinely good in a way that could be sentimental but never quite is — the game earns his idealism by putting it under real pressure and not letting it come free. He is the series' argument that optimism is not naivety if you're willing to bleed for it.
