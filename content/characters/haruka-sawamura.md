## Background

Haruka Sawamura enters the series in *Yakuza Kiwami* as a young girl caught in the middle of the Tojo Clan's war over the missing ten billion yen. Her connection to the missing money, her missing mother, and the conspiracy surrounding her pull Kiryu — a man who has just walked out of prison and wants nothing more than to be left alone — into protecting her.

He never stops.

---

## The Kiryu-Haruka Relationship

The relationship between Kiryu and Haruka is the emotional architecture of the Kiryu saga. He is terrible at expressing care. She knows it anyway. Over the course of *Yakuza 3, 4, 5,* and *6*, their bond evolves from guardian-ward to something closer to father-daughter, and the series' most devastating sequences all involve moments when that bond is threatened.

Kiryu never calls himself her father. He doesn't have to.

---

## Yakuza 5: The Idol

In *Yakuza 5*, Haruka becomes a playable protagonist for the first time — pursuing a career as an idol singer in Nagoya. Her chapters are a genuine departure: rhythm game sequences, social management, and the uncomfortable realities of the entertainment industry. It's the series asking what happens when the girl at the centre of everything is given her own story.

Her arc in *Yakuza 5* ends in an act of courage that shapes the entire franchise going forward.

---

## Yakuza 6

By *Yakuza 6*, Haruka is an adult, and the story she's left behind includes a son whose father the world doesn't know. Her condition at the game's opening — hospitalised, unable to speak — is the engine of Kiryu's final journey. That she gets to wake up and choose her own future is the ending the character earned.
