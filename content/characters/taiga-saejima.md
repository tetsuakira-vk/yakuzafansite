## Background

In 1985, Taiga Saejima walked alone into a Ueno Seiwa Clan gathering and killed eighteen men on orders from his patriarch. He was convicted of all eighteen murders and sentenced to death. He spent the next eighteen years on death row, waiting.

He never received a single visitor. His patriarch — the man who ordered the hit — never came.

---

## Character

Saejima is built like a wall and moves like one. In combat, he is the slowest and most powerful of the *Yakuza 4* protagonists — a brawler who doesn't dodge so much as absorb damage and keep walking. His fighting reflects his personality: immovable, deliberate, and when finally pushed to his limit, catastrophic.

He is also one of the most morally straightforward characters in the series. He knows what he did and why. He is not trying to escape it. He is trying to understand what it cost and whether any part of what he lived for was real.

---

## Connection to Majima

Saejima and Majima are sworn brothers — a bond that predates both their disasters. Majima's loyalty to Saejima, maintained across nearly two decades of silence and separation, is one of the deepest relationships in the series. Their reunion in *Yakuza 4* is earned in a way few moments in the franchise are.

---

## Yakuza 5

*Yakuza 5* gives Saejima his richest arc: escaped from prison a second time, navigating the yakuza underworld in Sapporo, and eventually confronting the full truth of the 1985 massacre. His chapters include mountain survival and bear hunting sequences that feel genuinely tense and isolated in a way that captures his character perfectly.
