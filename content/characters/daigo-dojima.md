## Background

Daigo Dojima is the son of **Sohei Dojima** — the Dojima Family patriarch whose murder (and the coverup of that murder) is the event that sets the entire Kiryu saga in motion. He grew up knowing his father's name was both a throne and a curse.

Kiryu first encounters Daigo in *Yakuza Kiwami 2*, where he is young, reckless, and trying to prove himself outside his father's shadow. Kiryu sees in him the same thing everyone who knew Sohei Dojima sees: potential, danger, and the question of which one wins.

---

## As Chairman

The Tojo Clan's Sixth Chairman is not a position Daigo wanted in the simple sense. He wanted to be worthy of it — to be the chairman the clan needed, not the chairman he inherited his right to be. The tension between those two things drives his arc across *Y3, 4, 5*, and *6*.

He makes mistakes. He gets captured, betrayed, and played. He makes decisions that protect the clan at the cost of individual lives. He also, repeatedly, does the right thing when the easier path would be to do nothing.

---

## Relationship with Kiryu

Daigo is the closest the series has to a figure who holds institutional power and uses it to protect rather than exploit. His loyalty to Kiryu — complicated by hierarchy, history, and the fact that Kiryu refuses to operate within any institutional structure — is one of the series' long-running emotional threads.

When *Like a Dragon Gaiden* makes explicit the cost Kiryu paid to protect the people around him, including Daigo's world, the weight of that debt is fully visible.
