## Background

Shun Akiyama runs **Sky Finance** — a loan company in Kamurocho with an unusual policy: he will lend to anyone, with no collateral, if they can pass a test of his devising. The tests vary. They are all designed to see if the person asking has something genuine to fight for.

He became wealthy through luck — literally, by surviving an event that destroyed everything he had and being found with a briefcase full of cash in the aftermath. He decided to use it to give people the same chance he got: one break, when the world says no.

---

## Character

Akiyama is the easiest Yakuza protagonist to like on first meeting and the most surprising to understand fully. The casual confidence and easy generosity read as surface charm until you understand where they come from: a man who genuinely has no attachment to the money, because the money isn't the point.

His fighting style is the most distinctive of the *Yakuza 4* protagonists — acrobatic, kick-focused, bouncing off walls and enemies with a fluidity that matches his personality. He doesn't plant himself and brawl. He moves.

---

## Yakuza 4

In *Yakuza 4*, Akiyama is the first protagonist, and his chapter establishes the tone: streetwise, funny, slightly baffled by how deep the conspiracy goes, and fully committed once he understands what's at stake. His relationship with Hana, his assistant, is one of the game's best running threads.

---

## Yakuza 5

Akiyama returns in Osaka, following the money through the web of conspiracies connecting all five protagonists' stories. His presence is smaller than in *Y4* but his competence is undiminished.
