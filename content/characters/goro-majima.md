## Background

Goro Majima's defining moment came before any game in the series: his exile. Once a full yakuza operative in the Shimano Family, Majima failed a hit ordered by his patriarch and was punished with imprisonment and torture by the Shimano Family. He lost his left eye. He was released into probationary exile — forbidden from yakuza activity — and set up running a cabaret club in Sotenbori, Osaka.

That period is the Majima we meet in *Yakuza 0*: a man stripped of everything, operating in a performance of normalcy while maintaining rigorous control over his actual feelings. Then he meets Makoto Makimura, the blind woman he's ordered to kill. And everything changes.

---

## The Performance of Madness

The "Mad Dog" persona — the knife, the eyepatch, the howling, the attacks from inside trash cans — is partly genuine and partly constructed. After his exile and imprisonment, Majima understood that the yakuza world rewarded unpredictability. He built a reputation for mania because a man with no boundaries is a man to be feared, and fear is protection.

Underneath it: a precise moral compass, genuine loyalty to those who earn it, and a personal code about what makes a fight meaningful. He doesn't hurt people who don't deserve it. He tests people he respects, sometimes very painfully.

---

## The Majima-Kiryu Dynamic

Majima's obsession with Kiryu — hunting him across Kamurocho, attacking him from nowhere, pushing him to his limits — is among the most analysed relationships in the series. The surface reading is a man addicted to finding a worthy opponent. The deeper reading, established by *Yakuza 0*, is something more complicated: two men who met at a pivotal moment and shaped each other's futures without ever fully acknowledging it.

---

## Combat

Majima is defined by unpredictability in combat. His four styles in *Yakuza 0* (Thug, Slugger, Breaker, and later Demon) embody different philosophies. In *Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii*, as the sole protagonist, he fights as **Sea Dog** (heavy cutlass) and **Mad Dog of the Seas** (acrobatic pistol-sabre). Both styles carry his signature — overwhelming, committed, joyful.

## Key Story Beats

- **Y0**: Ordered to kill a blind woman. Refuses. Protects her instead. Accepts exile's end knowing what it will cost.
- **Kiwami**: The Majima Everywhere system — hunting Kiryu across the city to push him back to full strength. A twisted kindness.
- **Kiwami 2**: His own playable campaign reveals what he was doing during Kiryu's Osaka trip.
- **Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii**: Amnesiac protagonist. Hawaii. Pirates. The series at its most Majima.
