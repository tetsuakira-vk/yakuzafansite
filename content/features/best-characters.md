## The Top Tier

### 1. Goro Majima
There is no contest. Goro Majima is the most electrically written character in the series and one of the most distinctive figures in action gaming. The gap between Majima-as-he-appears (erratic, violent, performing chaos) and Majima-as-he-is (a man of code, loyalty, and suppressed grief) is the most interesting interior space in the franchise. Yakuza 0 is largely his story. Everything after is a victory lap.

### 2. Kazuma Kiryu
The Dragon of Dojima is the series' foundation and the character whose development across six mainline games (and cameos across several more) constitutes one of the more complete arcs in the medium. Kiryu is stubbornly honourable in a world designed to make honour impossible, and watching him navigate that over twenty years of in-universe time is the reason the series exists. His return in Infinite Wealth is the series treating its own history with unusual care.

### 3. Ichiban Kasuga
A different kind of protagonist — where Kiryu is reserved, Ichiban is effusive; where Kiryu fights alone, Ichiban builds a party. His Dragon Quest-filtered worldview (the tendency to see life as an RPG with heroes and bosses) is the engine of the Like a Dragon era and it's better for it. The character avoids the usual "new protagonist after a beloved one" trap by not trying to replace Kiryu but complement him.

## Antagonists

### Ryuji Goda — *Yakuza Kiwami 2*
The only man in the series who meets Kiryu as an outright equal and knows it. The Dragon of Kansai wants the same thing as Kiryu — a world where strength is honest and loyalty means something — and the tragedy is that the structures they serve make that impossible. The final confrontation between them is the series at its most operatic.

### Yoshitaka Mine — *Yakuza 3*
Mine spends most of Yakuza 3 as an apparent obstacle and reveals himself, near the end, as something considerably more complicated. His motivation is one of the more human things the series has asked an antagonist to carry.

### Masato Arakawa — *Yakuza: Like a Dragon*
The central antagonist of the Ichiban era's first chapter. The connection between Arakawa and Ichiban is the emotional spine of Like a Dragon, and the reveal of what that connection means lands with more force than most mainline series finales.

## The Essential Supporting Cast

### Taiga Saejima
A man who spent eighteen years in prison for an act of loyalty, only to discover the loyalty was exploited. Saejima is the series' most physically imposing character and its most quietly honourable. His bear-wrestling substory in Yakuza 5 should not work and completely does.

### Shun Akiyama
Sky Finance's proprietor gives loans to people nobody else will help, and asks only that they prove themselves worthy. Akiyama is the series' smoothest character and the only protagonist who feels like he's enjoying himself. His arc in Yakuza 4 is the series' best "lone genius" story.

### Haruka Sawamura
Kiryu's ward, his reason to stop fighting, and the character whose development across Yakuza 3, 5, and 6 constitutes the series' most patient piece of long-form storytelling. Her idol arc in Yakuza 5 is the single most tonally unexpected thing in the franchise.

### Yu Nanba
The former nurse living homeless in Yokohama who joins Ichiban's party. Nanba is the party member who provides the series' most useful function: he believes in Ichiban before anyone else does, without it being saccharine about it. His personal backstory is quietly devastating.

### Akira Nishikiyama
What makes Nishikiyama one of the series' most memorable antagonists is that you meet him in Yakuza 0 as a generous, warm young man. The man he becomes is the direct result of a specific choice in a specific moment, and the tragedy is that the choice was almost understandable. One of the few antagonists in the series who is genuinely sad rather than just menacing.

## Honourable Mentions

**Daigo Dojima** — the Sixth Chairman who never wanted the throne and spent his career trying to deserve it. A recurring presence across the Kiryu saga who gets his best material in the later entries.

**Kiryu's orphanage children (Yakuza 3)** — collectively one of the series' greatest achievements in making you care about a group of minor characters quickly.

**The Florist of Kamurocho** — an information broker who appears in some capacity across almost every entry and remains completely inscrutable throughout.

**Tianyou Zhao** — *Yakuza: Like a Dragon.* A crime lord with a more complex relationship to his own history than the game initially lets on.

**Chitose Fujinomiya** — *Like a Dragon Gaiden.* One of the best new characters introduced in the shorter entries, with a backstory the game earns the right to tell.
