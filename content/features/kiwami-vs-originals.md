## The Short Answer

**Play the Kiwami versions.** If you are coming to these games now, in the mid-2020s, Kiwami 1 and Kiwami 2 are the correct versions of these stories. The original PS2/PS3 games are historical artefacts with the full narrative intact, but there is no practical reason to choose them over the remakes unless you specifically want the original experience.

---

## Yakuza 1 vs Yakuza Kiwami

### What Kiwami Added

**Majima Everywhere** — The most significant addition. In Kiwami, Goro Majima ambushes Kiryu constantly across the city, in disguise, to force him to fight and restore his combat skills after a decade in prison. This system doesn't exist in the original. It adds a meta-layer of comedy and character to a game that originally had Majima in only a handful of scenes. It is responsible for turning Yakuza Kiwami from a competent but dated remake into something genuinely worth playing.

**Dragon Style progression** — In the original, Kiryu's signature fighting style is fully available from the start. In Kiwami, Dragon Style is deliberately weakened at the beginning and restored through the Majima Everywhere encounters. This creates a sense of progression the original lacked.

**New substories** — Several substories were added to Kiwami that weren't in the original, fleshing out side characters introduced in Yakuza 0 who hadn't been conceived when the original launched in 2005.

**Updated engine** — Kiwami uses the Kiwami engine (not Dragon Engine), which looks and plays significantly better than the PS2 original or its PS3 remaster.

### What the Original Has

**Atmosphere** — The original Yakuza has a rawer quality that the remake smooths over. If you want to understand where the series came from, the original communicates it.

**Different script pacing** — Some players find the original's story pacing slightly different from Kiwami's. The core story is the same but the feel varies.

### Verdict
**Kiwami.** The Majima Everywhere system alone justifies the choice, and the improved combat makes the original's feel unnecessary.

---

## Yakuza 2 vs Yakuza Kiwami 2

### What Kiwami 2 Added

**Dragon Engine** — Kiwami 2 is the Dragon Engine's debut title. The jump from the Yakuza 2 PS3 remaster to Kiwami 2 is larger than any other remake in the series. Characters look current-generation. Indoor/outdoor transitions are seamless. Sotenbori is fully rebuilt.

**Majima Saga** — Kiwami 2 adds an entirely new playable chapter featuring Majima, set concurrent with the main story, that answers questions about his actions in the game. It is unique to Kiwami 2 and constitutes about 3–4 hours of play.

**Expanded side content** — New substories and activities not in the original.

**Remade music** — The soundtrack is largely re-recorded, with mixed reception. Some players prefer the original compositions.

### What the Original Has

**Original soundtrack** — Yakuza 2 on PS2/PS3 has music that some fans consider irreplaceable. Kiwami 2 replaces most of it.

### Verdict
**Kiwami 2**, overwhelmingly. The Dragon Engine transformation is significant, and the Majima Saga alone makes it the definitive version.

---

## What About Yakuza 3, 4, and 5?

These three games have been remastered (upscaled assets, trophy support, additional content restoration) but not fully remade. They run the original PS3 engine.

**Yakuza 3 Remastered** — The significant addition over the original is the restoration of content cut from the Western PS3 release: 17 substories, the hostess club, and disc golf. The 2019 remaster includes all of it. Kiwami 3 (2026) is a full Dragon Engine rebuild and the definitive version going forward.

**Yakuza 4 and 5 Remastered** — Both include all previously region-locked content and run in 1080p. No full remakes planned as of 2025.

---

## Yakuza 3 Remastered vs Yakuza Kiwami 3

If you're starting now, **wait for Kiwami 3** (released February 2026). It rebuilds Yakuza 3 in the Dragon Engine with expanded content including the new Dark Ties chapter featuring Mine. There is no reason to play the remaster if Kiwami 3 is available.
