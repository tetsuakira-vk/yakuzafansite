> Rankings are subjective. This is one take. A well-informed, considered take — but one take. The comment section on Reddit will disagree with at least four of these placements. That's correct behaviour.

---

## S Tier — Essential

### Yakuza 0
The series peak for most fans and the easiest recommendation in gaming. Two protagonists at the beginning of their legends, a story that earns every beat of its runtime, and the Bubble Economy setting that makes Kamurocho feel genuinely alive. The Real Estate and Cabaret Club management games alone justify the price of entry. If you play one Yakuza game, it's this one.

### Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth
The most ambitious game RGG Studio has ever made. It takes the Ichiban formula established in Yakuza: Like a Dragon, expands it to Hawaii, brings Kiryu back for what is essentially his final arc, and somehow sticks the landing for both protagonists. The aloha aesthetic is earned. The creature-collection minigame (Sujimon) is its own 20-hour adventure. The ending is one of the best in the series.

## A Tier — Excellent

### Yakuza Kiwami 2
The Dragon Engine's debut and still one of its best-looking entries. The Kiryu-Goda rivalry is the series' most compelling antagonist dynamic, Sotenbori is a joy to inhabit, and the side content is excellent. The story wraps with a rooftop finale that's hard to forget.

### Like a Dragon: Ishin!
The Bakumatsu setting is a genuine departure and it works. The sword-based combat is the most technically satisfying in the series. Seeing familiar faces cast in historical roles is a constant low-key delight. Often overlooked because of its Japan-only origins — don't make that mistake.

### Yakuza 5 Remastered
The most content in any single Yakuza game. Five protagonists, five cities, and a wildly ambitious scope that almost holds together. Kiryu's taxi driver arc is a slow burn that pays off enormously. Haruka's idol storyline is the most tonally unexpected thing in the series and it works. Long, occasionally exhausting, and essential.

### Like a Dragon Gaiden
Lean, focused, and the best pure action game in the series since the early Dragon Engine entries. Kiryu at his most confident and the Sotenbori/Castle setting is one of the best environments RGG has built. Short enough to not overstay its welcome. Required before Infinite Wealth.

## B Tier — Good

### Yakuza: Like a Dragon
A bold pivot to turn-based combat that divided the fanbase and was correct to do it. Ichiban Kasuga is a worthy protagonist. Yokohama is a great new city. The combat system takes time to click but rewards patience. Some pacing issues in the middle act and the villain reveal is telegraphed. A strong foundation for the Ichiban era.

### Yakuza 4 Remastered
The four-protagonist structure is more coherent than Yakuza 5's five, and each character's arc is strong independently. Tanimura's story covers genuinely dark thematic territory. The convergence finale is satisfying. Not the first game you'd recommend to a newcomer but essential for understanding the Tojo Clan politics.

### Yakuza Kiwami
A faithful remake of the 2005 original with the Majima Everywhere system grafted on. The story is the weakest in the Kiryu saga (the original game was the series figuring itself out) but it's required reading and the Majima additions make it significantly more enjoyable than it has any right to be.

### Judgment
A tighter, more focused mystery than most mainline entries. Yagami is a compelling protagonist. The detective mechanics add variety. Its main weakness is the follow sections, which drag. But the case it builds toward is genuinely good and the finale earns it.

---

## C Tier — Fine, With Caveats

### Yakuza 6: The Song of Life
Yakuza 6 is one of the most emotionally affecting entries in the series and simultaneously one of the weaker game-as-game designs. The Dragon Engine debut was rough (combat is shallow by the series' own standards, the side content thin). The story is magnificent if you've earned it. As a game, it's carried entirely by its narrative weight.

### Yakuza 3 Remastered
A game with a genuinely warm emotional core (the orphanage, the children, Okinawa) that is often let down by its combat, which was already dated at release. The side content restoration in the remaster helps. The antagonist plot is the series at its most convoluted. Worth playing for Kiryu's journey and not much else.

### Lost Judgment
Mechanically Judgment's superior in almost every way. Narratively it introduces a school-bullying subplot that drags the pacing across its second act. The Yagami-Kuwana dynamic is excellent. The ending is divisive. Recommended if you loved Judgment, optional otherwise.

### Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii
More spin-off than mainline — shorter, lighter, and makes no pretension of carrying the series' emotional weight. Majima is always entertaining. The pirate gimmick commits to the bit. A palate cleanser between major entries.
