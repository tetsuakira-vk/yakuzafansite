## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### Akiyama's Secret Loan Cases
Sky Finance has seven "impossible cases" — clients with zero creditworthiness who appear only if you've failed Akiyama's standard loan tests three times in a row. These are unlisted in the substory menu and each involves a genuinely interesting side story about someone Kamurocho has written off. One involves a former baseball player whose story quietly sets up a character who appears in *Yakuza 5*.

### Saejima's Prison Network
During Saejima's prison chapter, other inmates will pass information if Saejima has raised his reputation within the prison hierarchy. The full information network reveals the names of four former Saejima Family members whose fates are mentioned in passing in *Yakuza 0* — a retroactive connection RGG Studio added to make the timeline coherent.

### The Rooftop Route
Tanimura is the only protagonist with a rooftop traversal ability (climbing exterior pipes on specific buildings). The rooftop network across Kamurocho connects to two locations never accessible any other way: a small shrine with a permanent accessory pickup, and an observation point that triggers an optional but important piece of backstory about the Empty Lot (yes, still that).

### The Coliseum Challenger Queue
The Coliseum has an undocumented queue system: if you beat the champion with a specific protagonist, that protagonist becomes unavailable for 24 in-game hours while the next protagonist takes the title opportunity. Cycling all four protagonists through the Coliseum in the correct order — Akiyama, Tanimura, Saejima, Kiryu — unlocks a fifth opponent who only appears in this specific scenario and drops the game's best weapon.

## Puzzle Solutions

### The Mahjong Terminal: Rookie to Advanced
Most players ignore the Mahjong terminal (accessible through Akiyama's chapter). The trick for advancement:
- Never discard honour tiles in the first three turns — they appear worthless but are frequently in opponents' hands
- The AI in the first bracket always breaks on a Tsumo, never on a Ron. Play defensively until you can force a Tsumo hand
- The "Tutorial Mahjong" option in the menu has a hidden advanced mode accessible after completing all five beginner lessons

### Tanimura's Negotiation Minigame
Tanimura's unique mechanic — negotiating with NPCs to extract information — has a hidden optimal response tree. The rule: always choose the aggressive option first (it raises the NPC's suspicion meter), then immediately back down (suspicion drops below baseline, making them more cooperative than if you'd been polite from the start). This works on every NPC in the game.

### Saejima's Prison Fights: Who to Fight First
The prison fight order matters: take on the Infirmary enforcer before the Kitchen enforcer. The Infirmary enforcer drops a shiv that gives +20% attack in all subsequent prison fights — this item is removed when you leave the prison but it makes every remaining fight trivial.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Yakuza 4* was the first game in the series to use **multiple playable perspectives on the same location**. The same streets of Kamurocho look different depending on which protagonist is walking them — Akiyama sees a city of possibility, Saejima sees a cage.
- **Masayoshi Tanimura's combat style** (dirty street fighting, using environment and tricks) was developed after player feedback that Kiryu's Dragon Style felt too dominant. The developers wanted a protagonist who could be beaten by a strong opponent if the player wasn't careful.
- The **prison chapter** was based on research visits to Japanese correctional facilities, including interviews with both guards and former inmates. Several plot details about the prison's internal social structure were drawn directly from those interviews.
- *Yakuza 4* introduced **in-game time** as a mechanical feature — certain substories and encounters only appear at specific hours. This was later refined in subsequent entries.
- **Shun Akiyama's hair** — the gravity-defying swept style — was designed specifically to make him visually distinctive in a crowd shot with three other protagonists who all have more conventional appearances.
- The roof of the **Millennium Tower** (accessible in Tanimura's chapter) has a hidden platform that overlooks all of Kamurocho. The developers included it simply as a reward for players who explored.
