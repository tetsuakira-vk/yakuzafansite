## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Alternate Verdict Path
Judgment's courtroom sequences have a second, harder resolution path that most players never find. After gathering all standard evidence in a chapter, there's a secondary interview chain — triggered by revisiting a previously-interviewed witness with a specific topic from the case notes — that reveals additional evidence. Presenting this evidence during the trial unlocks a verdict variation with different dialogue that recontextualises the antagonist's motivation.

### The Old Lawyer's Files
In Yagami's law office, the filing cabinet in the back room (accessible throughout the game) gains new contents after specific story chapters. The files are labelled with case numbers and contain brief summaries of old Tojo Clan-adjacent legal cases — cross-referenced against dates, these are clearly cases from the *Yakuza 3–5* era, seen from a lawyer's perspective. It's a view of the series' events from outside the criminal hierarchy.

### Kaito's Training Ground
Behind the Champion District building, accessible only with the drone (unlocked in Chapter 4), a rooftop area shows clear signs of regular use — worn padding, a water bottle, practice targets. The prop placement implies Kaito trains here alone regularly. Speaking to Kaito about training after discovering the area triggers a unique conversation not otherwise available in the game.

### The Cat in the Evidence Room
The Kamurocho Police Department evidence room (accessible during Chapter 6) has a cat asleep on a filing cabinet in the back corner. It cannot be interacted with. It has no plot function. It appears to have been there long enough that the evidence room staff have left a water bowl for it. The cat does not move in any playthrough.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Drone Racing: Track Memorisation Shortcuts
The drone racing minigame is harder than it looks because the camera angle doesn't match the drone's actual trajectory. The rule: look at the shadow of the drone, not the drone itself. The shadow gives true ground position independent of the camera angle. On every track, the shadow is the reliable guide for gap clearance.

### The Mortal Reversal Fighting Skill
Yagami's Crane and Tiger styles have an advanced technique — Mortal Reversal — that the tutorial skips. It's activated by holding the dodge button through an enemy attack (not pressing it to dodge) — the timing window is tighter than standard dodge but the counter does critical damage regardless of enemy guard state. Mastering it makes the hardest fights in the game manageable.

### Investigation Minigame: Optimal Evidence Chain
The hidden object and investigation scenes have a non-obvious rule: examine every object in a room before selecting anything as "key evidence." The game doesn't tell you, but examining all objects first unlocks a bonus observation available only when Yagami has full context. Missing objects means missing potential evidence tags. This is especially important in the Chapters 5 and 8 crime scenes.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Judgment* was the **first game in the Yakuza universe built around a non-Yakuza protagonist** — Yagami is a defence lawyer turned private detective, operating in Kamurocho from the outside. The change in perspective was deliberate: the same city, seen from across the thin blue line.
- **Takuya Kimura**, one of Japan's most famous entertainers (former SMAP member, film actor), voices and provides facial motion capture for Yagami. His involvement was a significant media event in Japan — Kimura's casting brought the game to mainstream Japanese entertainment press coverage.
- The **legal thriller framing** required the development team to research Japanese criminal procedure extensively. The courtroom sequences reflect actual Japanese trial structure, including the mixed judge-jury (saiban-in) system introduced in 2009.
- **Kaito's backstory** — a former Yakuza enforcer who chose Yagami over his gang — was written as a counterpoint to every "loyalty to the organisation" narrative in the mainline series. He made a different choice, and the game takes his side.
- The game reuses **Kamurocho from Dragon Engine** but builds entirely new interiors for Yagami-specific locations: his office, the law firm, the court building. These spaces are detailed to a degree that suggests the team enjoyed building places that weren't nightclubs or Yakuza offices.
- *Judgment* received a **PS5/Xbox/PC remaster** in 2021. The original PS4 version features a different voice actor for a supporting character (due to a drug scandal involving the original cast member) — the PS5 version re-recorded those scenes.
