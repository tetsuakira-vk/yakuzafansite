The Yakuza series is famous for the gap between its main story — yakuza vendettas, dying family members, corporate conspiracies — and the sheer volume of completely unrelated activities available between story beats. These aren't filler. Several of them are as well-designed as standalone games.

---

## Music

### Karaoke
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2, Yakuza 3–6, Ishin!, IW, most spin-offs

The most iconic activity in the series. Kiryu (and other protagonists) sing J-pop tracks in rhythm-game style at karaoke booths. The songs are original compositions recorded specifically for the games, and some of them are genuinely excellent. *Baka Mitai* (Yakuza 0/Kiwami) became a viral meme in 2020 and introduced the series to an entirely new audience.

Each game has its own setlist. Yakuza 5 has the most tracks. Yakuza 6's *Smile Again* is a serious tearjerker in context.

### Hostess Club / Cabaret Club
**Present in:** Yakuza 0 (full management sim), Kiwami, Kiwami 2, some remastered titles

Yakuza 0's Cabaret Club Grand Prix is a full management simulation — hire hostesses, manage their schedules, train their skills, and compete against rival clubs. It's one of the deepest minigame systems in the series and can absorb many hours. Later appearances are scaled back conversational minigames with individual hostesses.

## Traditional Japanese Games

### Mahjong
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2, Y3–Y6, Gaiden, Infinite Wealth

Full four-player riichi mahjong. The series has been consistent in including a complete, properly-implemented mahjong system. If you don't know how to play mahjong and want to learn, the Yakuza series is a reasonable place to do it — the AI is beatable and the rules are all there.

### Shogi
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, some remastered titles

Japanese chess. Presented with full rules and a competent AI opponent.

### Cee-lo / Dice Games
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Y3–Y6

Gambling minigames using dice. Yakuza 0's gambling dens are particularly atmospheric.

### Oicho-kabu / Blackjack
**Present in:** Various across the series

Traditional Japanese card gambling and Western blackjack appear in casinos and gambling dens across the series.

## Sports

### Batting Centre
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2, Y3–Y6, Ishin! equivalent

Timing-based baseball batting. Difficulty scales across courses. Completing batting centre challenges is often required for substories or character-building items.

### Golf
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami 2, Y3, Y5, Infinite Wealth

Full golf holes with driver, iron, and putter mechanics. Yakuza 0's golf is surprisingly complete. Infinite Wealth takes it furthest with a dedicated golf course and tournament structure.

### Bowling
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Y3–Y6

Standard bowling. Yakuza 0's bowling is the most fleshed out with proper lane mechanics and spin control.

### Pool / Billiards
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2

8-ball pool with proper aim and power mechanics.

### Darts
**Present in:** Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2, Y4, Infinite Wealth

Standard darts with multiple game modes.

## Unique to Specific Games

### Pocket Circuit Racing — *Yakuza 0*
RC car racing. Build and upgrade cars with different stats, then race them in a dedicated tournament circuit. Far deeper than it has any right to be. Has its own full substory chain and is one of the most beloved activities in the game.

### Dragon Kart — *Yakuza: Like a Dragon*
A Mario Kart-style kart racing minigame set in Yokohama. Includes a tournament and fully upgradeable karts.

### Crazy Delivery — *Yakuza: Like a Dragon*
Ichiban takes a job as a delivery driver navigating Ijincho on a bicycle. Chaotic and funny.

### Can Quest / Aloha Quest — *Infinite Wealth*
A full Pokémon-style creature-collecting minigame within Infinite Wealth. Has its own story, evolution chains, and battle system.

### Real Estate / Business Management — *Yakuza 0*
Kiryu's storyline in Yakuza 0 involves taking over and managing properties in Kamurocho. Buy buildings, upgrade them, defeat rival managers, and generate passive income. A full business sim embedded in the game.

### Substory Photography / Gravure — *Yakuza 5*
A photography minigame where you scout and photograph models. Part of a substory chain but has its own mechanics.

## Arcades

### Club Sega
**Present in:** Almost every game

Playable SEGA arcade machines appear in every entry. Fully playable versions of *Super Monkey Ball*, *Virtua Fighter 2 & 5*, *Puyo Puyo*, *Sonic the Fighters*, *Fantasy Zone*, and others depending on the entry. Yakuza 0 has some of the best arcade offerings — vintage 1988-appropriate machines including *Space Harrier* and *Out Run*.

## Tips for Minigames

- **Don't skip them for substories.** Many substories require you to beat a minigame challenge to progress.
- **Mahjong and shogi have tutorials.** Use them. They're good.
- **Pocket Circuit in Y0 is mandatory for completion but genuinely fun.** Approach it as a proper game within a game.
- **Karaoke songs unlock gradually.** Return to karaoke booths across the game rather than doing everything in one session.
- **Casino activities can be exploited** via save-scumming if you need specific items. The games know this and it's fine.
