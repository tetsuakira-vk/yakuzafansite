## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Crew Manifest Hidden Entries
The pirate crew roster has 40 listed recruitment slots. Filling all 40 triggers an unlisted 41st entry: a crew member whose recruitment conversation reveals a connection to Majima's pre-*Yakuza 0* history — a period the series has never depicted. The conversation is short and Majima deflects most of it, but it's the most backstory-adjacent content he's been given outside of *Yakuza 0* itself.

### Majima's Recovered Memories System
Majima starts the game with amnesia (a deliberate comedic setup). His memories return in fragments triggered by specific environmental interactions — smelling gunpowder near a specific island, hearing a specific song in a port bar, seeing a map formation that echoes Kamurocho's street grid. None of the memory triggers are marked. They're purely environmental, and the memories themselves are presented as brief, jarring flashes that aren't commented on.

### The Real Pirate Historical References
The rival pirate captains Majima encounters are loosely based on real figures from the Golden Age of Piracy and from East Asian pirate history — the Wako pirates who operated in Japanese and Chinese waters during the 16th–17th centuries. The crew names, ship designs, and the trade route geography in the game draw from documented historical sources rather than generic pirate fantasy.

### The Kamurocho Flotsam
In the ocean between the main Hawaiian islands, a specific search pattern (using the ship's lookout system) uncovers a debris field containing items clearly marked as originating from Japan. One piece of debris — a cracked plate — has the Morning Glory Orphanage crest on the underside. It's a deliberate Easter egg with no plot function whatsoever.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Ship Combat: Boarding vs. Sinking
The game never explicitly tells you that boarding an enemy ship yields better loot than sinking it. The boarding mini-encounter (a short fight on the enemy deck) awards equipment and rare materials; sinking awards only gold and common supplies. Prioritise boarding for rare resource acquisition, sinking only when speed is the priority.

### The Treasure Map Cipher
The treasure maps acquired through the exploration system use a consistent cipher: the compass rose on each map is rotated 90° clockwise from true north, and distances are in units of 100m rather than the scale bar's apparent measurement. Correcting for both gives exact locations. Most treasure maps seem impossible until you apply these two corrections.

### Crew Morale: Fastest Build Method
Crew morale (which affects ship combat effectiveness) builds fastest through victories in the voluntary "challenge" ship encounters marked in red on the sea map — not through story missions. The red encounters are harder but award triple the morale per encounter. Morale doesn't decay between sessions, so farming red encounters before story chapters is always the optimal order.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii* was announced and released within a relatively short development window — significantly faster than mainline entries. The team has described it as a "high-energy side project" that let them experiment with systems they couldn't put in a mainline entry.
- **Majima as the protagonist** of a full spin-off was something the fanbase had requested for years. The pirate framing was specifically chosen because it let Majima operate entirely outside the Yakuza/Tojo Clan power structures that constrain him in mainline games.
- The **ship combat system** is a full mechanical layer — not a mini-game but a complete alternate gameplay mode with its own progression, crew stats, and tactical options. The development team cited classic naval strategy games as references.
- **Hawaii reappears** from *Infinite Wealth*, but the map is expanded significantly — new islands are accessible that weren't part of the previous game's geography. The continuity between the two games is explicit and referenced in the story.
- Majima's **amnesia conceit** is played largely for comedy early on but becomes genuinely affecting by the finale. The development team noted that amnesia let them tell a Majima story without being bound to the continuity of his established history.
- The game was released in **February 2025**, with simultaneous global release. It was the fastest turnaround between mainline entry and spin-off in the series' history.
