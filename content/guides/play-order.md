## The Short Answer

**Start with Yakuza 0.** It is a prequel set in 1988 and the best entry point the series has ever had. It introduces Kazuma Kiryu and Goro Majima, has the best tutorials, and features some of the strongest writing in the franchise. After that, follow release order.

---

## Recommended Play Order

| # | Game | Why |
|---|------|-----|
| 1 | **Yakuza 0** | Best starting point. Prequel to everything, introduces the world. |
| 2 | **Yakuza Kiwami** | Direct continuation. Covers events of the original Yakuza 1. |
| 3 | **Yakuza Kiwami 2** | Follows immediately from Kiwami. Covers Yakuza 2. |
| 4 | **Yakuza 3 Remastered** | Kiryu's third chapter. Okinawa arc begins. |
| 5 | **Yakuza 4 Remastered** | Four protagonists. Expands the world significantly. |
| 6 | **Yakuza 5 Remastered** | Five protagonists. The most ambitious Kiryu-era game. |
| 7 | **Yakuza 6: The Song of Life** | The end of Kiryu's story. Do not skip straight here. |
| 8 | **Like a Dragon: Ishin!** | Optional. Standalone period piece set in 1860s Japan. Can be played any time. |
| 9 | **Yakuza: Like a Dragon** | New protagonist, new city, new battle system. The Ichiban era begins. |
| 10 | **Like a Dragon Gaiden** | Bridges the gap between Yakuza 6 and Infinite Wealth. |
| 11 | **Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth** | Current endpoint of the series. Both protagonists. |
| 12 | **Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii** | Spin-off following Infinite Wealth. Standalone enough to play any time after IW. |

## The Judgment Series (Separate Continuity)

**Judgment** and **Lost Judgment** follow Takayuki Yagami, a defence attorney in Kamurocho. They share the world and city but are narratively independent from the mainline series. You can play them at any point — no Yakuza knowledge required. If you want context for the setting, play after Yakuza 0 at minimum.

---

## Can I Skip Games?

### Kiwami 1 & 2
Technically skippable if you read a summary, but not recommended. Kiwami 1 in particular sets up characters and grudges that pay off across the entire Kiryu saga.

### Yakuza 3, 4, and 5
These three form the long middle of Kiryu's story. Yakuza 6 will land emotionally if and only if you've spent time with the people Kiryu is protecting. Skipping them makes the ending feel hollow.

### Like a Dragon: Gaiden
Gaiden is short (15–20 hours) and almost mandatory for understanding Kiryu's emotional state going into Infinite Wealth. Play it.

### Like a Dragon: Ishin!
Fully optional. A standalone historical action game set during the Bakumatsu period. Characters are played by familiar faces from the main series but the story has no connection. Worth it if you enjoy the combat and Japanese history.

---

## What About the Original PS2/PS3 Games?

The original Yakuza 1 and 2 (PS2/PS3/PS3 remaster) are largely superseded by Kiwami 1 and Kiwami 2. Kiwami adds content, updates the engine, and includes the story intact. You do not need to play the originals unless you want to experience a specific version for historical reasons.

---

## Quick Reference: Chronological vs Release Order

The games are not set in the order they were released. Chronologically:

1. Like a Dragon: Ishin! (1860s — standalone)
2. Yakuza 0 (1988)
3. Yakuza Kiwami (2005)
4. Yakuza Kiwami 2 (2006)
5. Yakuza 3 (2009–2010)
6. Yakuza 4 (2010)
7. Judgment (2018)
8. Yakuza 5 (2012) *(see note)*
9. Lost Judgment (2021)
10. Yakuza 6 (2016)
11. Yakuza: Like a Dragon (2023)
12. Like a Dragon Gaiden (2023)
13. Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth (2024)
14. Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii (2025)

> **Note:** Yakuza 5 is set in 2012 but most of the Judgment cases overlap or occur adjacent to the gap between Y5 and Y6. The ordering above reflects in-universe events as best as the timeline allows.

**Recommended:** Play in release order, not chronological order. The series was designed that way. Yakuza 0 as an entry point works specifically because you already know the broad strokes of who Kiryu and Majima become — playing it chronologically first removes that dramatic irony.
