## The Full Timeline

The series spans from feudal Japan to the mid-2020s. This is where each game sits **in-universe**, not when it was released.

---

### 1860s — The Bakumatsu Period

**Like a Dragon: Ishin!** *(2023 remake of 2014 Japan-only original)*

Set during Japan's Bakumatsu period — the turbulent years of the late Edo era when the samurai class collapsed and the country modernised. The protagonist is Sakamoto Ryoma, a real historical figure, played by the motion-captured likeness of Kazuma Kiryu. Other familiar faces from the main series appear in-universe-anachronistic roles.

*Narrative connection to main series:* None. Fully standalone.

---

### 1988 — The Bubble Economy

**Yakuza 0** *(2015 / 2017 worldwide)*

The foundation of everything. Set in Kamurocho, Tokyo and Sotenbori, Osaka during Japan's economic bubble. A 20-year-old Kazuma Kiryu and 24-year-old Goro Majima navigate the early years of their criminal careers before either has the reputation they'll carry into the later games.

*Why it's the recommended starting point:* Playing Yakuza 0 after the rest of the series would give you dramatic irony — you know exactly who these men become. But it works magnificently as an introduction because the world and tone are established with a clarity that the later entries assume.

---

### 2005 — The Dojima Incident, Ten Years On

**Yakuza Kiwami** *(2016 / 2017 worldwide)*

Kiryu has served a 10-year prison sentence for a murder he didn't commit. He returns to Kamurocho to find the city changed, the Tojo Clan fractured, and a young girl named Haruka Sawamura who may be connected to ten billion missing yen. A remake of the original 2005 Yakuza.

---

### 2006 — Osaka

**Yakuza Kiwami 2** *(2017 / 2018 worldwide)*

One year after Kiwami. Kiryu travels to Osaka to prevent a war between Tokyo's Tojo Clan and Osaka's Omi Alliance. Introduces Ryuji Goda, the Dragon of Kansai — and Kamurocho Hills, a landmark that anchors the skyline of the series from this point forward.

---

### 2009–2010 — Okinawa

**Yakuza 3 Remastered** / **Yakuza Kiwami 3** *(2026)*

Kiryu has retired. He runs the Morning Glory Orphanage on the Okinawan coast with a group of children, including Haruka. A government resort development project and old enemies drag him back into the world he tried to leave. The only mainline Kiryu game to spend equal time outside Kamurocho.

---

### 2010 — Four Sides of Kamurocho

**Yakuza 4 Remastered** *(2019)*

Four protagonists — Kiryu, Shun Akiyama, Taiga Saejima, and Masayoshi Tanimura — whose stories converge on a conspiracy buried in a 25-year-old massacre. The most structurally ambitious game in the series up to this point.

---

### 2012 — Five Cities, Five Protagonists

**Yakuza 5 Remastered** *(2019)*

The most sprawling game of the Kiryu era. Five protagonists, five cities (Kamurocho, Sotenbori, Fukuoka, Nagoya, Sapporo), and the most substory content in any single Yakuza game. Kiryu is working as a taxi driver in Fukuoka under an assumed identity when events pull him back.

---

### 2018–2019 — The Yagami Cases

**Judgment** *(2018 / 2019 worldwide)*

Private detective Takayuki Yagami investigates a serial murder case tied to a pharmaceutical conspiracy in Kamurocho. Set in the same city as the mainline games, during the same period, but with no direct plot connection. A standalone entry that can be enjoyed with zero series knowledge.

**Lost Judgment** *(2021)*

Yagami investigates a school bullying case and a suspected murder in both Kamurocho and Yokohama. Directly follows the events of Judgment.

---

### 2016 — The Song of Life

**Yakuza 6: The Song of Life** *(2016 / 2018 worldwide)*

The final chapter of Kiryu's story. He emerges from prison to find Haruka, now an adult, in a coma — and a baby boy whose father is unknown. Set across Kamurocho and the fictional Onomichi, Hiroshima. Emotional payoff requires the full investment of games 0 through 5.

---

### 2023 — Yokohama

**Yakuza: Like a Dragon** *(2020)*

A new protagonist: Ichiban Kasuga, an enthusiastic low-level yakuza who models himself on Dragon Quest heroes. Betrayed and left for dead in Yokohama's Isezaki Ijincho district, he builds a party of misfits and fights his way up from the bottom. The shift to turn-based combat reflects Ichiban's Dragon Quest obsession in-universe.

---

### 2023 — Between the Lines

**Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name** *(2023)*

Set between Yakuza 6 and Infinite Wealth. Kiryu, living under an assumed identity, is forced back into action at a private island resort. A shorter, focused entry (~15 hours) that bridges the emotional gap between the two protagonists' arcs.

---

### 2024 — Hawaii

**Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth** *(2024)*

Both protagonists. Ichiban travels to Honolulu following a social media manhunt; Kiryu follows his own agenda, dealing with a terminal diagnosis. The largest game in the series. The convergence of the Kiryu and Ichiban storylines.

---

### 2025 — Hawaii, Continued

**Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii** *(2025)*

A spin-off following Goro Majima in Hawaii after the events of Infinite Wealth. Shorter and more focused than the mainline entries. Standalone enough to enjoy without the full series context, though the character beat lands harder if you've played IW.

---

## Why Play in Release Order, Not Chronological Order?

The games were **written** in release order. Yakuza 0 works as a prequel specifically because you know what becomes of Kiryu and Majima — their choices in 1988 carry weight because you know the men they'll spend the next 20 years becoming. Watching a 20-year-old Kiryu nervously clutch his principles is poignant precisely because you've already seen him defending those same principles in his 30s and 40s.

Playing chronologically strips that dramatic irony. Recommended route: **Yakuza 0 first** (which is also the recommended play-order starting point), then follow release order from Kiwami onwards.
