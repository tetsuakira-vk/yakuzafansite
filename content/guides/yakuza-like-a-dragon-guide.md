## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Dragon Quest Tribute System
Ichiban's Dragon Quest obsession isn't just character flavour — it's wired into the battle system's visual language. When Ichiban levels up, the animation and sound effect deliberately echo the Dragon Quest level-up sequence. Several of the job class icons are direct visual references to Dragon Quest job symbols. The "Heat Action" equivalents — called Party Finishers — have names that reference specific Dragon Quest spells when translated literally.

### Ijincho's Hidden Backalley Bar
In the northwest quadrant of Isezaki Ijincho (Yokohama), a narrow alley off the main entertainment strip has a bar with no signage. It can only be entered after completing a specific substory involving a debt collector (unlocked in Chapter 6). The bar's regular customer is a character who connects several of the party members' backstories and who never appears in the main narrative.

### The Eri Kamataki Fourth Wall Moment
During one of Eri Kamataki's (the party's optional business manager) substory conversations, she mentions a game she's been playing that is described, in exact mechanical terms, as *Yakuza 5*'s hostess club minigame. She then says she finds turn-based combat "a little slow." It's the most self-aware moment in the game and easy to miss if you skip dialogue.

### Homeless Camp Details
The Kotown homeless community that Ichiban works with is rendered with unusual care — each resident has a name, a background, and multiple dialogue evolutions as the story progresses. At a specific point in the story (after Chapter 9), revisiting the camp and speaking to every resident triggers a communal scene that doesn't appear in the main quest log and provides essential context for Ichiban's backstory.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Job Class Optimal Progression
The job system has a hidden interaction most players miss: **mastering a job class carries a stat bonus to your next class**. The optimal early game path for Ichiban:

1. Level Hero to rank 10 (fast, starting class) — carries Strength bonus
2. Switch to Foreman for ranks 10–20 — carries Defence bonus
3. Return to Hero with the stacked stats — now substantially stronger than if you'd stayed Hero throughout

### Sotenbori Battle Arena: Floor 30 Strategy
The Battle Arena's 30th floor has a set of three simultaneous enemies that most parties can't survive on first encounter. The solve: bring a full Breaker + Hostess + Foreman + Chef composition. The Breaker's area knockback prevents the enemies from using their combo attack. The Chef's cooking buffs stack per turn — enter the floor with three pre-cooked meals in the inventory.

### Business Management: Fastest Profit Route
The management minigame profits scale with employee skills, not investment size. Counterintuitively: hire the lowest-rank employees first and train them up rather than buying high-rank hires. Trained employees have a hidden loyalty stat that doubles sales during competition events. Buying in the electronics district first unlocks a competitor whose defeat unlocks the highest-margin district in the game.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- **Ichiban Kasuga's name** contains a deliberate pun: "Ichiban" means "number one" in Japanese — a joke the character is completely sincere about. His surname "Kasuga" is a common Japanese family name, making him an ordinary person with an extraordinary self-image.
- The **turn-based combat system** was a genuine risk. After 20 years of action combat, the switch divided the fanbase before release. Producer Masayoshi Yokoyama has said in interviews that the decision was non-negotiable — Ichiban's Dragon Quest obsession *had* to be reflected mechanically.
- *Like a Dragon* features a **deliberate tonal shift from the mainline series**: more ensemble comedy, broader cultural commentary (Ichiban's time in jail, his experiences as a day labourer), and explicit discussions of class, race, and belonging in Japanese society through the Kotown storyline.
- **Yokohama's Isezaki Ijincho** is a real district, faithfully reproduced. The area has a significant Korean and Chinese population in real life — the game's Kotown storyline engages directly with that social reality.
- The game was released in January 2020 in Japan. The **Western release was the same year (November 2020)** — one of the fastest turnarounds in series history, partly driven by the series' growing international audience.
- **Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio** has confirmed that the turn-based system is permanent for Ichiban's storylines. Kiryu's gameplay, in *Infinite Wealth*, returned to action combat — the two systems coexist as reflections of each protagonist's perspective.
