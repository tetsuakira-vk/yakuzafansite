## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Real Mr. Shakedown Route
The seven Mr. Shakedown encounters roaming Kamurocho and Sotenbori respawn after defeating them and grow stronger each time — but they also drop exponentially more money. The final confrontation with the highest-level Shakedown can yield over 10 million yen in a single fight, making it the fastest late-game cash farming method once your combat is strong enough to reliably beat him.

### Underground Stores
Both cities have underground item shops accessible only through specific substory chains. These sell the Dragon & Tiger accessories — the most powerful items in the game — and are easy to miss entirely on a first playthrough since they require completing a chain of substories that don't obviously connect.

### The Hidden Bar Encounter
In Sotenbori, the bar directly behind the Cabaret Grand hides a recurring character encounter in the back booth only available late at night (in-game time). This feeds into one of the more affecting substory chains and is frequently missed because the timing window is narrow.

### Disco EX Songs
All three disco venues (including Club Maharaja Sotenbori) have unlockable EX difficulty songs that don't appear until you've SS-ranked every normal song in that venue. The EX songs require perfect timing across much longer sequences and unlock special scenes with the venue's hostess character.

### Makoto Makimura's Chapter Callbacks
During Majima's storyline, several environmental details in Sotenbori — the specific koi pond, the arrangement of lanterns near the Shofukucho West entrance — are callbacks that pay off in the final Majima chapter and are easy to miss as foreshadowing on a first play.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Pocket Circuit: Optimal Car Builds by Track
- **Dream Machine Circuit** (high speed, gentle curves): Max Motor + Balanced Tuning + Wide Grip Tyres. Focus on top speed over handling.
- **Nightmare Circuit** (tight corners): Trade top speed for Stiff Frame + High Grip Tyres. The AI loses time on late braking; you win by cornering clean.
- **Final Circuit**: You need both speed AND handling. Use the Carbon Frame once unlocked, max the motor, and use Wide Grip. There's no trick — it's the best parts available.

### Real Estate Boss Optimal Sequence (Kiryu)
Take the Maharaja district first — it yields the highest baseline income and the first boss fight introduces the combat upgrade loop early. Then work south to Tenkaichi. Leave the Champion District last; the boss is significantly harder but the property income boost is largest.

### Cabaret Grand Optimal Hostess Training (Majima)
Prioritise Charm upgrades first, then Mood. Style is the cheapest stat to raise and costs you proportionally the most at lower hostess levels. Always run the S-rank lessons for your highest-performing hostess before a Grand Prix event, not beforehand — the training bonuses apply immediately.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- **Kamurocho is directly based on Kabukicho**, the entertainment district in Shinjuku, Tokyo. The street grid, several building facades, and even the colour temperature of the signs are accurately reproduced. Walking through Kabukicho with Y0 in mind is an uncanny experience.
- **Sotenbori is based on Dotonbori/Namba**, Osaka. The canal, the famous crab restaurant sign (reproduced as a dragon sign in-game), and the dense back-alley structure are all there.
- *Yakuza 0* was the first game in the series to release on PC in the West before a console version. It became the best-selling entry in the West on launch.
- The **1988 bubble economy setting** was chosen specifically because it let RGG Studio justify the absurd amount of cash flying around. Yen amounts that would be ludicrous in a modern setting were economically plausible in 1988 Japan.
- **Majima's cabaret backstory** was invented specifically for this game — in prior games he was simply always "Mad Dog." Y0 is technically the origin story of an origin story.
- The **Pocket Circuit circuit-racing minigame** was a real hobby subculture in Japan during the late 1980s. The game's depiction of it is more accurate to actual 1980s slot-car culture than most people realise.
- There's a **Michael Jackson tribute** in the game: a substory features a character clearly modelled on MJ performing a Thriller-style dance routine. The developers have confirmed it was deliberate.
