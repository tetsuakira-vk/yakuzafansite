## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The School Stories Parallel Narrative
The School Stories (extracurricular club sidequests at Seiryo High) have a hidden connective thread. Completing four or more club storylines in any order unlocks a conversation with a student NPC who reveals that the clubs are all aware of each other's situations — and several of the club advisors are connected through a shared history the main school story never mentions. The conversation is accessed by speaking to a student in the library (not the club rooms) after Chapter 7.

### The Skateboard Physics Secrets
The skateboard traversal system has undocumented trick combinations. In Isezaki Ijincho, the long descending slope near the port has a ramp at the base that, approached at full speed during certain trick animations, launches Yagami to a normally-inaccessible rooftop. The rooftop has a stash and a view of the harbour that includes a ship with a Tojo Clan marking — visible through binoculars Yagami carries but rarely uses.

### Mastermind's Hidden Photo Archive
In the Mastermind's base of operations (late game, Chapter 10+), a secondary door requires a code found only by examining three specific environmental details earlier in the game — details that aren't marked as interactive. The room behind the door contains a photo archive with images that directly reference events from *Judgment*'s story, implying the Mastermind had a longer-term plan than the Chapter 1 framing suggests.

### Kaito's Dance Session
At a specific point in Chapter 6, Kaito can be found in a back room of the Social Club engaged in a private dance practice session. The game prompts nothing — it's discovered only by entering a room you have no reason to enter at that chapter point. Watching without interrupting triggers a scene where Kaito acknowledges Yagami saw him and the two have a genuinely funny exchange. If you enter too quickly, the scene doesn't trigger.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Bike Chase: Optimal Route Through Chapter 5
The motorbike chase sequence in Chapter 5 has a shortcut through a side alley on the second corner that cuts 8 seconds from the chase and bypasses the vehicle roadblock. The alley entrance is narrow enough that most players steer away from it instinctively. Taking it requires hard left input before it's visually obvious — hitting the visual cue is already too late.

### Boxing Gym: Fast XP Method
The boxing gym's sparring system (social club activity) has an XP multiplier triggered by winning with specific finishing moves rather than running down the timer. A hook-to-body-to-hook-to-uppercut combo that triggers the "Precision Finish" badge awards 3x XP over a standard win. The badge is hidden in the sparring menu under "performance bonuses."

### School Stories: The Dance Club Final Performance
The Dance Club's final performance sequence is a rhythm game with a timing quirk: the song's tempo changes at the 2-minute mark and the beat indicators don't visually communicate the shift. The change is audible about 4 beats before the display catches up. Players who follow the display fail here; players who follow their ears pass consistently.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Lost Judgment* expands the setting to **Yokohama's Isezaki Ijincho** — the same district as *Like a Dragon* (Ichiban's game). The two games share a city but approach it differently: Ichiban's Yokohama is street-level, day-labourer, working class; Yagami's Yokohama reaches into high schools, law firms, and the city's institutional power.
- The **School Stories** were the result of direct player feedback on *Judgment*'s side content: players wanted more social/human stories. The school club format let the writers explore bullying, academic pressure, and adolescent isolation in a way that's unusually direct for the series.
- **The skateboarding traversal** was added to distinguish Yagami's movement through Yokohama from both Ichiban's (on foot) and Kiryu's (on foot, different era). The team wanted Yagami to feel lighter, faster, and more playful than either protagonist.
- The game deals explicitly with **Japan's statute of limitations on sexual assault** — at the time of the game's Japanese release, this was a live legal debate. The game takes a clear position. RGG Studio has described it as the sharpest direct social commentary in the series.
- **The DLC storyline** (*The Kaito Files*) plays an entire additional campaign as Kaito, providing backstory that recontextualises several of his *Lost Judgment* interactions. It's optional but notably it's the first time a supporting character received a full standalone campaign.
- *Lost Judgment* was the **last Judgment game released** — the future of the series was put on hold due to talent agency disputes over Takuya Kimura's likeness rights. Whether the series continues remains unresolved.
