## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Sujimon Professor's Hidden Research Notes
The Sujimon system — a deliberate Pokémon parody — has a layer beyond collecting. After registering every Sujimon in the Honolulu area, the Professor character reveals a secondary set of "legendary" Sujimon that can only be encountered at specific map locations during specific times of day. These aren't tracked in the Sujimon Dex until you've found the first one, which appears at the east beach at 2am in-game time.

### Kiryu's Health Tracker Easter Egg
Kiryu's ongoing health subplot is tracked in a way most players don't notice: his walking animation changes subtly over the course of the game as the story progresses. The animators built in a degradation system where his gait becomes slightly more laboured after specific story beats. Players who replay sections from the beginning notice the difference immediately; on a first playthrough it's nearly imperceptible.

### The Hotel Guest Registry
In the early game hotel sections, the guest registry desk (interactive prop in the lobby) contains a list of names. Cross-referencing against substory characters reveals that three of the listed guests are characters from *Yakuza 5* whose fates were ambiguous. The names are common enough to be deniable but the combination of first and surname for all three is specific enough to be deliberate.

### The Coconut Grove Time Capsule
On the third explorable island section, a marked-but-unexplained mound in a coconut grove can be dug up using Ichiban's farming tools (unlocked in Chapter 7). Inside is a box containing a letter written in Japanese, a photograph, and a coin. The letter is a message from a Japanese tourist who left it in 1988 — the same year as *Yakuza 0*. The photograph's background is recognisably the Sotenbori canal.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Palekana Job Class Combinations
The Hawaiian job class system has combinations that create synergy bonuses not shown in the menu:

- **Aquanaut + Chef**: The Aquanaut's water abilities trigger the Chef's "fresh catch" passive, doubling healing output on ocean-themed stages
- **Cabbie + Streetwalker**: Both classes have a +movement speed passive that stacks when both are in the party simultaneously — the fastest exploration setup in the game
- **Samurai + Kunoichi**: The Samurai's counter creates a trigger for the Kunoichi's follow-up strike. The combined counter-follow combination does more damage than either class's dedicated abilities

### Anaconda Golf: The Left Fade Correction
The golf minigame on the Anaconda course has a consistent physics anomaly on holes 4, 7, and 11: all three have a slight leftward pull regardless of the displayed wind direction. Correct by aiming two indicators right of target on these specific holes. The game never acknowledges this.

### Dungeons: The Loot Priority Rule
Dungeons have a hidden loot quality modifier based on the order of clearing rooms. Clearing the furthest room first (counter-intuitive, as it requires fighting through the dungeon twice) sets the dungeon's loot table to its highest tier. The room order doesn't affect drop rate — only loot table quality.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth* features **the largest playable map in series history**, with multiple Hawaiian islands reproduced at significant scale. The development team spent extended time in Hawaii doing location surveys, with an emphasis on the Japanese-American cultural layers of Honolulu.
- **Kiryu and Ichiban's parallel storylines** were developed by separate writing teams who coordinated only at chapter endpoints — this was a deliberate creative choice to ensure the two protagonists felt tonally distinct. Kiryu's scenes are noticeably more restrained and introspective than Ichiban's.
- The game has a **full English dub** — the first time the mainline series received a full voiced English script rather than subtitle-only localisation. The English voice cast includes well-known voice actors across the party lineup.
- **Hawaii's Japanese diaspora** is central to the story in a way that's historically grounded — the game engages with the specific history of Japanese immigration to Hawaii, internment during WWII, and the current relationship between Japanese-American and Japanese national communities.
- The **Sujimon system** was designed as a loving parody, but the underlying collection mechanics are fully developed — there are over 250 Sujimon to register, and the competitive battle system has tournament rankings. It's a complete side game within the game.
- *Infinite Wealth* was released in **January 2024 globally** — another simultaneous worldwide launch, cementing the series' new release strategy following the success of *Gaiden*'s simultaneous release.
