## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### All Majima Everywhere Disguises
Majima hunts Kiryu across the city in disguise. Finding and triggering each form unlocks Dragon Style upgrades. The full list:
- **Street Punk Majima** — randomly encountered on most streets after leaving the batting cages early
- **Taxi Driver Majima** — hail a cab. He may be the driver. He always charges the meter first.
- **Officer Majima** — hanging near Nakamichi and Tenkaichi after the first police station event
- **Goromi** — the hostess working at a specific club on Pink Street. Requests a date. Takes it very seriously.
- **Zombie Majima** — only appears during a late-game street area after a specific substory chain; attacks in a shambling horde ambush
- **Golf Majima** — near the batting cages, appears carrying clubs. The fight is in a parking structure.
- **Nowhere Man Majima** — only triggered by refusing to fight him three times in succession in earlier encounters. He appears at Serena looking disappointed.

### The Nishikiyama Flashback Scenes
The Kiwami remake added a series of playable Nishikiyama scenes not present in the original game. These are accessed via a specific door near the Tojo Clan HQ area and are easy to miss — there's no prompt. The flashbacks recontextualise his antagonist arc entirely and are unmissable narrative content.

### Hidden Telephone Club Numbers
The Telephone Club minigame has three unlisted numbers that lead to the same woman, progressively revealing more of her background story. These numbers are only accessible if you've called all standard numbers at least once and then dial the number shown in the substory "Wrong Number" in reverse.

### Champion District Back Alley Stash
Behind the Champion District arcade, between a dumpster and the wall (requires crouching camera angle to see the item glow), there's a stash point containing a Tauriner Maximum and a Tiger Binding — both useful early.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Dragon Style Unlock Priority
Dragon Style starts locked. Majima Everywhere is the unlock system. Priority order for fastest progression:
1. Street Punk encounters (appear earliest, easy fights)
2. Find Goromi at Club Shine on Pink Street (unlocks a unique Dragon gauge upgrade)
3. Complete the "Majima Under the Weather" substory to unlock the Golf encounter
4. Don't use Dragon Style moves until you have at least 3 gauge upgrades — the style scales dramatically with investment

### Coliseum Late-Game Strategy
The Bed of Straw fight (hardest optional battle) requires stockpiling Tauriner++ drinks and using the Essence of Corkscrew Blow as often as possible. The trick: the boss is vulnerable to environmental finishers from the arena wall. Back him toward it early.

### Substory "The Price of an Idol"
The taxi pickup points change each time you play it. The optimal route: Maharaja → Millennium Tower back entrance → Serena. Always take the right at Taihei and cut through the alley — the meter runs slower in alleys in this specific substory.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- **"Kiwami"** (極) means "extreme" or "the ultimate" in Japanese — the same character appears in the title of the Yakuza series itself (*Ryū ga Gotoku*, "like a dragon").
- The original *Yakuza* (2005) was developed in under two years and the team cut significant content. *Kiwami* restored much of it and the Nishikiyama chapters are almost entirely new.
- **Goro Majima's voice actor**, Hidenari Ugaki, has stated in interviews that Majima Everywhere was his favourite creative challenge — he had to perform the same character across radically different emotional registers (menace, farce, genuine sadness) often within the same session.
- The scene where **Goromi** (cross-dressed Majima) goes on a date with Kiryu at the restaurant is performed entirely straight, with Kiryu treating it with complete sincerity. It has become one of the most beloved moments in the series.
- *Yakuza Kiwami* was the first game in the series to use **binaural audio** for headphone users — spatial audio that made Kamurocho feel three-dimensional at night.
- A **Pocket Circuit racer** from Yakuza 0 appears as a substory character in Kiwami, now grown up and running the senior circuit.
