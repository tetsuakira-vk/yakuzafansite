## Is this series for me?

### What kind of game is Yakuza / Like a Dragon?
At its core it's a story-driven action RPG set in a fictionalised version of modern Japan, specifically the red-light districts of Tokyo, Osaka, and Yokohama. The mainline games follow members of the Japanese organised crime world navigating betrayal, honour, family, and increasingly absurd substories.

The tone swings wildly — a cutscene about a dying child can be followed by a minigame where you manage a hostess club or do battle in a chicken-racing tournament. That tonal whiplash is not a bug. It's the soul of the series.

### How long are the games?
Main story only: roughly 20–30 hours per game. With substories and side content: 50–80+ hours. Completionists can hit 100+ hours on the larger entries like Yakuza 5 and Infinite Wealth.

### Is it violent / adult content?
Yes. Yakuza games are rated 18/M. The combat is brutal, the story involves organised crime, and the games are set partially in red-light districts. There is no explicit sexual content but the games don't shy away from mature themes.

## Getting started

### Where do I begin?
**Yakuza 0.** It's a prequel set in 1988 and was designed partly as an entry point for new players. It introduces everything you need to know. See the [full play order guide](/guides/play-order/) for the recommended sequence after that.

### Do I need to play them in order?
For the Kiryu saga (Yakuza 0 through Yakuza 6) — yes, broadly. Characters, relationships, and plot threads build across all six games and the payoff in Yakuza 6 requires the emotional investment of the earlier entries.

Like a Dragon: Yakuza and onwards feature a new protagonist and are a softer reset. You can start the Ichiban era without the full Kiryu backstory, though knowing who Kiryu is makes it richer.

### Can I skip the older games?
Technically yes, but you'll be missing context that the games assume you have. A summary helps. Actually playing them is better. Yakuza 3–5 in particular are where most people make their "favourite game in the series" picks.

## Technical questions

### What platforms is it on?
Most of the series is available on **PS4, PS5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC (Steam)**. Yakuza 0 through Yakuza 6 are also on Xbox One. The older PS3 remastered collection (Y3–Y5) is PS4 only. Check individual game pages for exact platform availability.

### Is it on Game Pass?
As of 2024–2025: Yakuza 0, Kiwami, Kiwami 2, Yakuza 3–6 Remastered, and Like a Dragon: Ishin! have all appeared on Game Pass at various points. Library rotation varies by region — check the current Xbox Game Pass catalogue.

### Do I need a Japanese console or DLC for the original content?
No. The localised versions of every mainline game include the full Japanese content. Early Western releases of Yakuza 3 notoriously cut some substories, but the remaster (2019) and Kiwami 3 (2026) restore all of it.

### Is there English voice acting?
Yakuza 0 through Yakuza 6 are **Japanese audio only** with English subtitles. Yakuza: Like a Dragon introduced a full English dub, and subsequent entries (Ishin!, Gaiden, Infinite Wealth) also have dual audio options.

## Story questions (spoiler-free)

### Is the story actually good or is it just a meme?
It's genuinely good. The Kiryu saga across six games is one of the more complete character arcs in the medium — a story about a man trying to leave a violent world behind and being repeatedly dragged back by loyalty and circumstance. It earns its melodrama.

The Ichiban arc (Like a Dragon onwards) is more overtly comedic and referential but the emotional core is equally strong.

### Is it too confusing to follow?
The series has a lot of characters, factions, and political manoeuvring within the Tojo Clan. The broad strokes are always clear — who is doing what and why — but the specific clan politics take a few games to sink in. You don't need to memorise everything. The games recap key points.

### How serious is it vs how silly is it?
The main story is played almost entirely straight. Side content (substories) ranges from genuinely touching to completely ridiculous. The tonal balance is intentional and part of what makes the series distinctive. You will go from weeping in a cutscene to helping a man reclaim his stolen porn collection within the same hour.

### What is the difference between "Yakuza" and "Like a Dragon"?
In 2022 Sega rebranded the English title from "Yakuza" to "Like a Dragon" (mirroring the Japanese title *Ryu Ga Gotoku*) to create a clean distinction between the Kiryu-era games and the Ichiban-era games. The same studio makes all of them. The rebrand caused confusion — "Yakuza: Like a Dragon" is simply the first game in the Ichiban era, not a spin-off.

## Common concerns

### I heard the combat is repetitive — is that true?
The beat-em-up combat of the Kiryu games (Y0 through Gaiden) is repetitive by design — you are a one-man army and it feels like it. Each game adds mechanics to keep it fresh. The Ichiban games use a turn-based JRPG system which plays quite differently.

### Do I have to do the side content?
No. The main story is self-contained. Side content enriches the world but skipping it entirely is a valid choice. That said, the substories are often the best writing in the games. The minigames are genuinely good and some are worth hours of play on their own.

### The graphics look old on the early games — does it matter?
Yakuza 0 through Kiwami 2 use a pre-Dragon Engine that does look dated by 2020s standards. The storytelling and performance quality make it irrelevant within about 20 minutes. Yakuza 3 Remastered upscales the original PS3 assets which can look rough. Kiwami 3 (2026) rebuilds it in the Dragon Engine.
