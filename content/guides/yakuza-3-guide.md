## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Orphanage Kids' Hidden Requests
Each child at Morning Glory Orphanage has a secret favour that can only be triggered after reaching a specific relationship level with them — shown in the game by the child greeting Kiryu differently at the gate. These hidden requests aren't listed in the substory menu and each one yields a unique accessory. Completing all of them unlocks a post-credits scene that most players never see.

### Okinawa's Real Street Connections
The Ryukyu district in *Yakuza 3* is based on **Naha's Kokusai-dori** (International Street) and the surrounding alleys. Several real stores have counterparts in the game — the awamori distillery near the south beach is based on a real producer whose bottles appear on the shelves accurately labelled.

### The Beach Cave
On the far eastern edge of the Okinawa map, a cave entrance is visible but has no prompt. Returning during a specific weather condition (rain, only possible after Chapter 4) reveals the entrance opens — inside is a hidden item cache and a short scripted scene with one of the orphanage children that has no corresponding substory entry.

### The Restored Substories (Remaster Only)
The Western PS3 release cut 17 substories, the hostess club minigame, and the Disc Golf side activity. All were restored in the 2019 remaster. If you played the original Western release, you've never played about 25% of the side content — including "The Strongest Man in the World" chain, which contains what many fans consider the funniest cutscene in the entire series.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Underground Coliseum: The Unofficial Boss Rush
The Underground Coliseum in Kamurocho has a secret "boss rush" mode unlocked after completing the standard circuit twice: speak to the promoter and select the third dialogue option (which has no label). This pits Kiryu against every prior boss simultaneously in a wave format. The reward is the Brawler's Ring, which permanently boosts counter-attack damage.

### Disc Golf: Ryukyu Course Par Solutions
- **Hole 3** (the signature difficult hole): use the high-arc throw directly over the hill rather than trying to curve around it. The game's wind model always favours the high approach on this hole.
- **Hole 7**: aim 15° left of the basket to account for the persistent left drift that affects all throws on this hole regardless of in-game wind direction. (This appears to be a physics quirk rather than intentional design.)

### Hostess Club Optimal Hostess Order
Run the hostess with the highest Style stat first in each session — Style has a disproportionate effect on the opening mood assessment. Follow with Charm for sustained score. Never open with Mood; it's a sustain stat, not an opener.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Yakuza 3* was the first game in the series developed for PS3 from the ground up. The transition from PS2 required the team to essentially rebuild every system, which is part of why the game feels slower-paced than its predecessors.
- **Okinawa** was chosen as the second setting specifically because RGG Studio wanted to contrast Kamurocho's claustrophobic neon density with something open and sunlit. The tonal shift was deliberately jarring.
- The **children at Morning Glory Orphanage** are each based on archetypes drawn from actual research the development team conducted with Japanese child welfare organisations. Several staff members have noted in interviews that the children's storylines were the most emotionally demanding writing in the game.
- *Yakuza 3* was the first game in the series to show **Kiryu as a caretaker** — this decision fundamentally changed how Western audiences understood the character. Prior to Y3, many Western players saw Kiryu purely as an action protagonist.
- The **Underground Coliseum** in Kamurocho has appeared in every game set in Kamurocho from Yakuza 3 onwards — a continuous narrative thread across more than a decade of releases.
- *Yakuza 3* was so poorly localised in its original Western PS3 release that several substories were mistranslated so severely they made no narrative sense. The remaster's retranslation fixed all of them.
