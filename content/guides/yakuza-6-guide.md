## Secrets & Easter Eggs

### The Onomichi Cat Network
Onomichi has a network of cats scattered across the town — 16 in total — that can be photographed and fed. Most players find a handful. The secret: feeding all 16 cats with specific food items (purchased from the Onomichi convenience store, not the general vendor) triggers a scene where a local shopkeeper reveals the history of the area that directly connects to the Chapter 5 backstory reveal. It's one of the warmest scenes in the game and is skipped by almost everyone.

### Haruto's In-Game Reactions
Baby Haruto's behaviour is a passive easter egg system: he reacts to the environment. Carry him into a convenience store and he reaches for the snack shelf. Walk near loud music and he startles. Take him to the sea wall at night and he quiets. None of this affects gameplay — it's purely observational — but it required a significant amount of dedicated animation work that goes unnoticed.

### The Hostess Club Ghost Story
In the Kamurocho hostess club, late at night, a hostess will tell a ghost story during conversation that makes specific reference to events from *Yakuza 3* — including the name of a character who died in that game. The reference is oblique enough that it's easily taken as generic storytelling, but the name is too specific to be coincidental.

### The Real-Name Restaurant in Onomichi
The restaurant where Kiryu eats in Chapter 2 is based on a real establishment in Onomichi, Hiroshima. The dishes shown in the cutscene — especially the oyster set — are real menu items. The developers worked with the actual restaurant during production, and the restaurant briefly became a tourist destination after the game's release.

## Puzzle Solutions

### Clan Creator: Optimal High-Damage Formation
The final Clan Creator mission requires a specific formation almost no guide covers: place your two highest-attack Brawlers in the front row flanking a Support unit (not on the edges). The Support unit in the centre doubles the flanking Brawlers' attack speed. Place Weapon units in the back row to cover the Support unit from aerial enemy waves.

### Karaoke: Song Unlock Sequence
The karaoke minigame has three songs locked behind completion of specific substories. The fastest unlock sequence: complete "Old Friends" (accessible in Chapter 3 near the Onomichi docks) → "Night Lights" (Kamurocho Chapter 5) → "The Last Journey" (post-game Onomichi). Each unlocks immediately after the substory resolves.

### The Onomichi Fishing Spots
- **Best catch rate**: The east pier at dawn (the first available in-game time window). The bream spawn is densest here.
- **Rare fish**: The cave inlet north of town, accessible only by boat (unlock after Chapter 4). The rare hairtail only spawns here during storm weather.
- **Fishing contest win condition**: Enter with a confirmed bream of 40cm+, which can only be caught at the east pier. The contest opponent's fish never exceeds 38cm.

## Fun Facts & Trivia

- *Yakuza 6* was the **first game built entirely in the Dragon Engine**, RGG Studio's new in-house engine replacing the aging PS3-era system. The engine was designed around seamless interior/exterior transitions — no loading screens when entering buildings.
- **Onomichi** (Hiroshima Prefecture) was chosen as the second city because it represented something Kamurocho never could: a small, slow, deeply traditional town where everyone knows everyone. The contrast with Tokyo was the point.
- The game features **Beat Takeshi** (Takeshi Kitano), one of Japan's most famous directors and actors, as a major character. His involvement was considered a significant cultural moment — Kitano had never appeared in a game of this type before.
- **Kiryu's arc** in this game was originally intended to be his permanent farewell to the series. The ending was written as a definitive conclusion. His reappearance in *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth* was a later creative decision.
- The **Dragon Engine's crowd system** in Yakuza 6 was a genuine technical leap — pedestrian crowds in Kamurocho had individual pathfinding and reaction to Kiryu's presence. Prior games used simpler loop animations.
- *Yakuza 6* released in Japan in December 2016 but **Western release was delayed until April 2018**. The delay was partly due to localisation scope and partly due to the transition between PS3-era and PS4-era Yakuza fanbases in the West.
