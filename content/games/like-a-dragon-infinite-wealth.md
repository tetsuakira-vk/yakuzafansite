## Story

**Ichiban Kasuga** is fired from his job after a viral smear campaign, and a social media lead suggests his long-absent mother may be in Hawaii. He goes looking for her — and finds a conspiracy larger than he imagined, involving a tech billionaire with a plan to reshape criminal society through an app called *Palekana*.

**Kazuma Kiryu** receives a diagnosis: cancer. Facing mortality, and having hidden himself from everyone who cared about him, he crosses paths with Ichiban in Honolulu. Their dynamic — old legend and new heart, stoic veteran and incandescent idealist — is the emotional core of the game.

*Infinite Wealth* is the series' most emotionally ambitious entry: a story about mortality, parenthood, legacy, and what you owe the people you've failed.

---

## Combat Evolved

The turn-based system from *Like a Dragon* is substantially expanded:

- **Palekana dungeon** — a massive multi-floor challenge dungeon with roguelite elements
- **Free movement** — characters can now move within a radius before attacking, enabling flanks, hazards, and positioning
- **New jobs** — expanded job system with dozens of new classes and inter-party combo attacks
- **Sujimon** — a Pokémon-inspired sub-system where you capture and battle enemy types

---

## Hawaii as a Setting

Honolulu is the largest explorable city in the series — a dense urban grid with beaches, shopping districts, bar strips, residential neighborhoods, and a coastal waterfront. The contrast with Kamurocho's neon claustrophobia is intentional. Hawaii feels open, bright, and deceptively welcoming.

---

## The Kiryu Bond Episodes

Post-launch DLC added extended Bond Episodes for Kiryu dealing directly with his cancer storyline. The Kiryu episode in particular is considered some of the most affecting writing RGG Studio has produced.

---

## Reception

*Infinite Wealth* is widely regarded as the series' high watermark — a game that rewards long-term fans while remaining accessible, with a runtime over 80 hours for a thorough playthrough. Metacritic: **93 (PS5)**.
