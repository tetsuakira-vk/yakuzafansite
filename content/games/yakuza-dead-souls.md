## Story

A mysterious infection has overrun Kamurocho. The government quarantines the district, trapping its residents — civilians, yakuza, and whoever else didn't get out in time. Four men go in to deal with it.

*Dead Souls* (known in Japan as *Ryu ga Gotoku: Of the End*) is explicitly non-canon — a spin-off that takes the series' cast and drops them into a B-movie zombie scenario. It does not take itself seriously and is better for it. Majima in particular is in his element, enthusiastically hunting zombies with industrial equipment.

---

## Gameplay

Unlike the brawler mainline games, *Dead Souls* is a **third-person shooter** with cover mechanics. Each character has a unique weapon loadout and playstyle:

- **Akiyama** — balanced, fast, agile
- **Majima** — heavy weapons, explosive ordnance, no restraint
- **Goda** — shotguns and firepower, fighting with a prosthetic arm
- **Kiryu** — the last to be playable, with the broadest toolkit

The shooter mechanics are dated but the game's energy carries it.

---

## Why Play It

*Dead Souls* is for fans who already love the cast and want to see them in a gleefully stupid situation. It's not the place to start, and it's not pretending to be. But Majima mowing down undead hordes while cackling is a specific kind of Yakuza experience that nothing else delivers.

The game is **PS3 exclusive** and has never been remastered or ported. Physical copies or PS3 emulation are the only options.
