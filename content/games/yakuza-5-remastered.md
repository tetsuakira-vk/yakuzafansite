## Story

*Yakuza 5* is the culmination of the Kiryu saga as a multi-protagonist epic. The story opens with Kiryu working as a taxi driver in Fukuoka, having left the orphanage to protect Haruka from his yakuza associations. Haruka, meanwhile, is in Nagoya pursuing a dream of becoming an idol singer.

Five storylines unfold across Japan, each in a different city:

- **Kiryu** in Fukuoka, drawn back into the underworld through a conspiracy targeting the Tojo Clan
- **Saejima** in Sapporo, escaping prison again and navigating the northern yakuza landscape
- **Haruka** in Nagoya, navigating the brutal realities of the idol industry in a storyline unlike anything else in the series
- **Akiyama** in Osaka, following the money trail back to a shared enemy
- **Shinada** in Nagoya, a former baseball player investigating match-fixing and trying to clear his name

The Haruka chapters are particularly notable — a rhythm game / social simulation hybrid that captures the cutthroat idol world in ways that feel genuinely uncomfortable.

---

## Scale and Ambition

Yakuza 5 was the largest game RGG Studio had made. Five full explorable cities, each with their own substories, minigames, and atmosphere. The game's scope is staggering even by today's standards.

The Western release was delayed three years and eventually came as a digital exclusive — a sign of how uncertain SEGA was about Western demand. The Remastered version is the definitive way to play.

---

## Hunting and Taxi Minigames

Each protagonist has unique activities: Kiryu's taxi driving and mountain hunting systems are among the most fully realized minigame suites in the series. The bear hunting sequences in Hokkaido are legitimately tense.
