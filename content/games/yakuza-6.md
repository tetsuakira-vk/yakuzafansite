## Story

Kazuma Kiryu walks out of prison to find Haruka Sawamura — the girl he raised, the closest thing to a daughter he has ever had — in a coma after a hit-and-run, and an infant son whose father nobody knows.

This is where Kiryu's story ends. Not with a war. Not with a conspiracy. With an old man trying to find out who fathered an orphaned baby, in a quiet port town called Onomichi, Hiroshima, that turns out to have its own long-buried secrets.

*The Song of Life* is unlike most games in the series. The scale is deliberately smaller. The stakes are more personal. The tone is elegiac. It knows it is the final chapter of a story that has run for over a decade, and it earns its ending.

---

## Dragon Engine

*Yakuza 6* introduced the **Dragon Engine**, which became the foundation for all future RGG Studio releases. Key changes:
- No loading screen when encounters begin — enemies walk up and fighting starts seamlessly
- Fully destructible environments in combat
- Characters carry weight and physics in a way earlier games couldn't simulate
- Improved facial capture for emotional close-ups, crucial for this story's quieter moments

---

## Onomichi

The second city, Onomichi, is one of the series' most distinctive settings. An actual port town in Hiroshima Prefecture, preserved and quiet, with steep slopes, temples, and a tight-knit community suspicious of outsiders. It feels like nowhere else in the franchise.

---

## The Finale

*Yakuza 6* concludes the story of Kazuma Kiryu — though not permanently, as later games revisit his character. But as a designed conclusion, it is among the most emotionally affecting endings in the series. Bring tissues.

---

## Notable: The Clan Creator

The Clan Creator real-time strategy minigame, where you recruit actual Japanese celebrities (including members of the baseball team Hiroshima Toyo Carp), is peak Yakuza sideshow energy.
