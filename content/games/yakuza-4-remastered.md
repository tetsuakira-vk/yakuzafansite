## Story

*Yakuza 4* splits its story across four characters, each chapter revealed in sequence before converging in the final act.

**Shun Akiyama** — a money lender who lives by a strange code: he gives loans to anyone who passes his test, with no collateral. Behind his easygoing manner is a man who survived losing everything and rebuilt from nothing.

**Taiga Saejima** — a convicted murderer serving a life sentence for killing eighteen yakuza in a single night. As he escapes, the truth about that massacre begins to unravel.

**Masayoshi Tanimura** — a corrupt police officer investigating the death of his father, a cop killed years ago in Kamurocho. His search leads to a conspiracy at the heart of the Tojo Clan.

**Kazuma Kiryu** — the fourth chapter. By the time you reach Kiryu, you've seen Kamurocho from three different perspectives, and the final act pulls all threads together.

---

## Why Four Protagonists Works

RGG Studio used the multi-protagonist format to show the same city and the same events from radically different social positions. Akiyama moves through high society. Saejima through the criminal underworld. Tanimura through the corruption of law enforcement. Each reveals something the others can't.

The structure was ambitious enough that the studio returned to it in *Yakuza 5*, this time with five protagonists and five cities.
