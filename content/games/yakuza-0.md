## Story

*Yakuza 0* is set in December 1988, during the height of Japan's economic bubble. The story follows two protagonists across parallel narratives:

**Kazuma Kiryu**, a low-level Yakuza operative in the Dojima Family, finds himself framed for a murder he didn't commit. The victim? A man connected to a vacant plot of land in Kamurocho known only as **The Empty Lot** — a parcel of real estate so valuable that every faction in the Tokyo underworld wants it. What begins as a desperate scramble to clear his name becomes a story about loyalty, sacrifice, and what it means to live by a code.

**Goro Majima**, the man who will one day be known as the *Mad Dog of Shimano*, is living a different kind of hell. Exiled from the Yakuza and working as the manager of a Sotenbori cabaret club, Majima is given a chance to return to the organization — but only if he carries out a single assassination. His target is a woman named Makoto Makimura. What he discovers when he finds her will change both of them.

## Why Play This First

*Yakuza 0* is the canonical starting point for the series, even though it was developed after Yakuza 1–5. It introduces the franchise's core mechanics, its two most beloved protagonists, and establishes the emotional stakes that carry through the entire saga. The 1988 bubble-era setting is also visually and musically extraordinary — neon-soaked and impossibly stylish.

## Gameplay

### Combat Styles

Each protagonist has **three combat styles** that can be switched on the fly:

**Kiryu**
- *Brawler* — balanced all-rounder, strong heat actions
- *Rush* — fast footwork, evasion-focused, high combo potential
- *Beast* — slow but devastating, uses environmental objects as weapons

**Majima**
- *Thug* — street brawler with dirty tricks and grabs
- *Slugger* — baseball bat-based, wide swings and crowd control
- *Breaker* — breakdancing-inspired, flashy and disorienting

A fourth style for each character is unlocked through the main story.

### Money and Progression

Set during the economic bubble, money flows freely — and is the primary progression currency. Defeating enemies drops cash directly. Both protagonists have money-making side businesses: Kiryu runs a **Real Estate empire** across Kamurocho, while Majima manages a **Cabaret Club** in Sotenbori. These minigames are surprisingly deep and serve as the main way to grind for combat upgrades.

---

## Side Content

*Yakuza 0* is legendary for its side content. Highlights include:

- **Substories (100+)** — self-contained vignette stories ranging from genuinely moving to completely absurd
- **Pocket Circuit** — competitive slot-car racing with a surprisingly involved upgrade system
- **Disco** — a rhythm minigame with its own progression and story
- **Telephone Club** — a 1980s-era dating simulator segment
- **Mahjong, Shogi, Bowling, Pool, Darts, Fishing** — all fully playable
- **Mr. Shakedown** — roaming boss encounters with massive money rewards

---

## Reception

*Yakuza 0* was a critical and commercial turning point for the Western release of the series. Its Metacritic score of **85 (PS4)** marked the franchise's mainstream breakthrough outside Japan, and Steam reviews remain "Overwhelmingly Positive." It is consistently cited as one of the best games of the 2010s.
