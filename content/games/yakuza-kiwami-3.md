## Overview

*Yakuza Kiwami 3 & Dark Ties* is the full Dragon Engine remake of *Yakuza 3* (2009), bundled with *Dark Ties* — a new original story chapter centred on antagonist Yoshitaka Mine. The Kiwami treatment brings the Okinawa-set Kiryu story up to the visual and mechanical standard of the current series while restoring and expanding content.

## What's New in the Kiwami Remake

### Full Dragon Engine Rebuild
*Yakuza 3*'s original PS3 engine is replaced entirely with the Dragon Engine, bringing seamless indoor/outdoor transitions, current-generation character models, and the improved combat system established in *Kiwami 2* and subsequent entries.

### Restored Content
The original Western PS3 release of *Yakuza 3* notoriously cut 17 substories, the hostess club minigame, and the Disc Golf activity. The 2019 remaster restored these; *Kiwami 3* rebuilds them fully and expands several substory chains.

### Dark Ties
The bundled *Dark Ties* chapter is a new story featuring Mine — the antagonist of *Yakuza 3* — told from his own perspective. It covers the events that shaped him before the main story and provides the character depth that the original 2009 release only gestured at. Dark Ties is exclusive to this package.

### Revised Combat
The Kiwami Repair system returns — Kiryu's combat abilities degrade when idle, encouraging active play — alongside the expanded Heat Action system and sub-weapon mechanics introduced across the Kiwami line.

## Setting

*Yakuza 3* is the only mainline Kiryu game to split its time equally between Kamurocho and a second city — Okinawa's fictionalised Ryukyu district, based on Naha's Kokusai-dori. The contrast between Kamurocho's neon density and Okinawa's open coastal light defines the game's tone. The Dragon Engine version of Ryukyu is the most detailed rendering of the setting in the series' history.

---

## Morning Glory Orphanage

Kiryu's adopted family at Morning Glory Orphanage returns as a central narrative focus. The Kiwami remake expands the individual children's storylines beyond the 2019 remaster's restoration, with new substories and the extended post-credits sequence that most players never saw in the original.
