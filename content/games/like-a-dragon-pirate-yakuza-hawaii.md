## Story

Goro Majima wakes up on a beach outside Honolulu with no memory of how he got there. The only clues: a treasure map tattooed on his chest and a child named Rich Komeki who found him washed up on shore.

This is the setup for a full pirate adventure. Majima assembles a crew, commands a ship, and sails between the islands of Hawaii while uncovering the truth about who erased his memory and why. The story sits alongside *Infinite Wealth* chronologically and references its events without requiring it as a prerequisite.

---

## Combat Styles

Majima fights with two styles:

- **Sea Dog** — a heavy cutlass-based brawler style with wide swings, grabs, and devastating finishers
- **Mad Dog of the Seas** — the chaotic acrobatic style from Majima's DNA, retooled with a sabre and pistol, unpredictable and aggressive

Both styles can be switched mid-combat.

---

## Naval Combat

The ship-to-ship combat system is a genuine standout. Broadside cannon exchanges, boarding actions, and crew deployment create a loop that sits comfortably between arcade approachability and real tactical depth. Upgrading your ship — the **Goatee** — is a primary progression path.

---

## Crew System

Majima recruits crewmates across the islands, each with unique ship combat abilities and substories. The crew dynamics give the game its emotional texture: *Pirate Yakuza* is, at its heart, a story about Majima finding people worth protecting.

---

## Tone

This game is exactly as ridiculous as it sounds and plays it completely straight. Majima gives impassioned speeches about the sea. There are sea monster encounters. There is a full maritime courtroom drama substory. It is peak RGG Studio.
