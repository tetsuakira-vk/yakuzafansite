## Story

After the events of *Yakuza 6*, Kiryu faked his death to protect his family and the orphans of Morning Glory. Operating under the alias **Joryu**, he works for the Daidoji Faction — a shadowy government-adjacent organisation — doing jobs that the official record cannot acknowledge.

When a high-profile kidnapping in Sotenbori threatens to destabilise the truce he's maintaining, Joryu is pulled into a confrontation that will test whether the Dragon of Dojima can truly stay buried.

*Gaiden* also sets up *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth*, including Kiryu's cancer diagnosis, which factors heavily into that game's emotional core.

---

## The Spider and the Yakuza Style

Kiryu fights with **two styles**:

- **Yakuza Style** — the classic Dragon of Dojima brawler, refined and brutal
- **Agent Style** — a new high-tech toolkit: explosive cigarettes, a grappling hook for aerial combat, deployable drones, and an extendable wire that launches enemies across the arena

Agent Style is the mechanical highlight. The gadget-based combat is inventive and lets Kiryu fight with a momentum and verticality the series hadn't had before.

---

## The Castle

The game's secondary hub is **The Castle** — a massive floating venue run by the mysterious Bacchus, where underground fighting tournaments draw contestants from across the criminal world. Winning tournaments unlocks upgrades, recruits allies, and reveals backstory.

The Castle is the game's most memorable original contribution to the series' setting vocabulary.

---

## Scale and Pacing

*Gaiden* is intentionally smaller than mainline entries. RGG Studio described it as a "side story" in scope. The main story is focused, fast-paced, and almost entirely free of filler. For players who found the mainline games' padding exhausting, *Gaiden* is a revelation — it never wastes a scene.

---

## Recommended Play Order

Play after *Yakuza 6* and before *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth*. The events and reveals bridge both games directly.
