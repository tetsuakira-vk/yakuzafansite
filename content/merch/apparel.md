## Official SEGA / RGG Studio Apparel

SEGA produces official branded clothing through its online store and at events. The range covers basics (T-shirts, hoodies) with game-specific graphic prints across the series.

**SEGA Official Shop (JP):** sega.jp/toys — the most direct source for current official apparel. Ships internationally with a forwarding service. Stock rotates with new releases and anniversary events.

---

## Notable Collaboration Lines

### UNIQLO UT Collaborations

UNIQLO's UT graphic T-shirt line has featured Yakuza / Like a Dragon in multiple collections. These are particularly notable because they:
- Are produced to UNIQLO's standard (reasonable quality for a graphic tee)
- Were sold internationally through UNIQLO stores globally
- Feature art sourced directly from SEGA, making them officially licensed

Previous UT collections have featured *Yakuza 0*, *Yakuza: Like a Dragon*, and *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth* designs. UNIQLO announces UT collaborations seasonally — the Yakuza series returns periodically. Worth monitoring if you want accessible, internationally available official clothing.

### Deus Ex Machina × Like a Dragon

A collaboration between RGG Studio and Japanese motorcycle apparel brand Deus Ex Machina produced a small run of branded riding-wear-adjacent pieces. Limited quantities, primarily Japan retail, now rare secondhand.

---

## Event Merchandise

**Tokyo Game Show and RGG Studio fan events** produce exclusive merchandise runs that aren't available through standard retail. These have included:

- Branded tote bags and pouches
- Enamel pins and badge sets (character portraits, the Dragon logo)
- Collaboration clothing with event branding

Event exclusives appear on Mercari Japan shortly after events as attendees sell duplicates or items they didn't want. This is the most reliable way to access them internationally.

---

## Fan and Third-Party Clothing

A significant market exists for fan-produced clothing through platforms like **Redbubble**, **TeePublic**, and **Etsy**. Quality and licensing status varies widely — none of these are official.

For officially licensed clothing that looks like it was designed by someone who understands the series rather than a marketing department, UNIQLO UT collaborations remain the standout recommendation.

---

## Sizing Notes

Japanese official apparel uses Japanese size standards, which run smaller than European or American equivalents. A Japanese L is typically a European/American M. Check size charts before ordering and factor in when buying through proxy services that can't easily handle returns.
