*Yakuza Kiwami 3 & Dark Ties* launched on February 12, 2026, on PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, Nintendo Switch 2, and PC via Steam.

The game rebuilds *Yakuza 3* (2009) entirely in the Dragon Engine — bringing Kiryu's Okinawa chapter up to the visual and mechanical standard of the current series — and bundles it with *Dark Ties*, an entirely new story chapter following *Yakuza 3* antagonist Yoshitaka Mine.

## Yakuza Kiwami 3

The Kiwami remake retells Kiryu's retirement arc: life at the Morning Glory Orphanage in Okinawa, the government resort development threatening the coastline, and the events that pull him back into the world he tried to leave. The Dragon Engine version is the most detailed rendering of the Ryukyu district setting in the series.

### New Fighting Styles
Kiryu has two styles in Kiwami 3:
- **Dragon of Dojima: Kiwami** — the ultimate evolution of his classic combat style
- **Ryukyu** — a weapons-based style drawing on traditional Okinawan martial arts, new to this entry

### Restored and Expanded Content
The original Western PS3 release cut 17 substories, the hostess club, and disc golf. The 2019 remaster restored them; Kiwami 3 rebuilds them in full and expands several substory chains. The orphanage children each have extended storylines beyond the remaster's restoration.

### New Activities
- **Life at Morning Glory** — expanded orphanage minigames with unlockable storylines
- **Bad Boy Dragon** — a gang-building activity new to this entry
- **12 playable Game Gear titles** — the first appearance of emulated Game Gear games in the franchise

## Dark Ties

Originally conceived as bonus content, *Dark Ties* evolved into a full campaign due to Mine's popularity with the fanbase. It covers Mine's rise through the yakuza ranks in 2007 — the events that shaped him before the main story of Yakuza 3, and the bond he formed with Tojo Clan chairman Daigo Dojima.

Mine is playable in his own combat system, including a **Dark Awakening** mode enabling powerful combo attacks. Producer Sakamoto described it as *"the darkest story out of all Like a Dragon entries."*

## Critical Reception

| Platform | Metacritic |
|----------|-----------|
| Nintendo Switch 2 | 77 |

| PlayStation 5 | 74 |

Reviews praised the improved combat and *Dark Ties*' narrative depth. Criticism centred on some removed content versus the remaster, visual presentation, and the relatively short length of the Dark Ties campaign.
