*Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii* released on February 21, 2025, and it is exactly as unhinged as the title suggests. Goro Majima — fan-favorite mad dog of Shimano, former patriarch of the Majima Family — wakes up on a beach with no memory, a treasure map, and an inexplicable new calling: becoming a pirate king.

## What Is This Game?

This is a direct spin-off to *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth*, set in the same Hawaiian archipelago. Rather than the turn-based RPG combat of the mainline series, *Pirate Yakuza* returns to the series' action RPG roots, with Majima cutting loose across high-seas ship battles, island exploration, and the kind of over-the-top boss fights RGG Studio is known for.

## What Works

The ship combat system is a genuine surprise. Broadside cannon volleys, boarding actions, and crew management create a loop that never gets old. Majima's dual fighting styles — **Sea Dog** (brute force cutlass) and **Mad Dog of the Seas** (chaotic acrobatic combat) — feel distinct and satisfying.

The writing is peak RGG absurdity. Within the first three hours you will witness Majima recruiting a talking parrot, challenging a local fisherman to an impromptu knife-throwing contest, and delivering a genuinely moving speech about the meaning of freedom. Classic.

## What Needs Work

The map density is lower than *Infinite Wealth* — Honolulu is impressive but the outer islands feel sparse in this early stretch. Side content takes a few hours to open up.

## Verdict So Far

If you have any love for Majima, this is unmissable. A bold, joyful spin-off that proves RGG Studio can do almost anything with these characters.
