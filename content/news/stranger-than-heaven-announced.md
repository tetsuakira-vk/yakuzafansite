RGG Studio revealed *Stranger Than Heaven* at the Xbox Partner Preview on March 26, 2026 — and it is unlike anything the studio has made before.

The game was previously known internally as **Project Century**. It is not a Yakuza or Like a Dragon game in the conventional sense: there is no Kiryu, no Ichiban, no Kamurocho neon. Instead, the studio is building a sprawling historical narrative set across five decades of Japanese history.

## What It Is

*Stranger Than Heaven* follows protagonist **Mako Daito**, who experiences fifty years of life — from 1915 to 1965 — ageing in real time as he lives through the social and political upheaval of early-to-mid 20th century Japan.

The five confirmed time periods:
- **1915** — Late Taisho era, early modernisation
- **1929** — The years of rising nationalism and economic crisis
- **1943** — Wartime Japan
- **1951** — Occupied Japan, post-war reconstruction
- **1965** — High-growth era, modern Japan taking shape

## The Kamurocho Connection

Footage shown at the Xbox Partner Preview includes recognisable versions of Kamurocho and Sotenbori — the series' signature cities — in their early-20th-century forms. This has fuelled significant community speculation that *Stranger Than Heaven* is an origin story for the Tojo Clan and the criminal underworld that underpins the entire Like a Dragon timeline.

RGG Studio has not confirmed or denied this connection.

## Platforms and Release Window

*Stranger Than Heaven* is targeting **PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC**. It will be available on **Xbox Game Pass** on launch day. No release date has been announced.

A full deep-dive presentation is scheduled for **May 6, 2026 at 4pm PDT / 7pm EDT**, where RGG Studio will detail the core mechanics of the time-period structure.

## What to Expect

RGG Studio's track record with historical settings is strong — *Like a Dragon: Ishin!* demonstrated the studio's ability to move outside the modern yakuza milieu while preserving the series' tonal identity. *Stranger Than Heaven* appears to be a significantly larger ambition: a genuine multi-era historical epic rather than a single-setting period piece.
