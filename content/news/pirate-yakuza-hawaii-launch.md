*Like a Dragon: Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii* is out today, February 21, 2025, on PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One, and PC via Steam.

The game puts Goro Majima — fan-favourite antagonist-turned-protagonist and series constant since Yakuza 0 — at the centre of a standalone spin-off set in the Hawaiian archipelago following the events of *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth*. Majima wakes up on a beach with no memory, befriends a young boy named Rich, and somehow ends up captaining a pirate crew.

## What's Different

This is an action game, not a turn-based RPG. Pirate Yakuza returns to the real-time brawler combat of the Kiryu era rather than the Dragon Quest-inspired system of the Ichiban games. Majima has two fighting styles — **Sea Dog** (cutlass-focused, hard-hitting) and **Mad Dog of the Seas** (the chaotic acrobatic style series veterans will recognise) — and the game adds high-seas ship combat as a new system.

## The Setting

The game shares the Hawaiian archipelago setting of *Infinite Wealth* and visits Honolulu alongside several fictional outer islands. It's shorter than a mainline entry — RGG Studio have positioned it as a focused spin-off in the vein of *Like a Dragon Gaiden*.

## Early Reception

Early reviews have been broadly positive, with critics praising the combat and Majima's writing while noting the world feels smaller than *Infinite Wealth*'s Honolulu. The ship battles have been singled out as a genuine surprise.
