*Like a Dragon Gaiden: The Man Who Erased His Name* launched on November 9, 2023, on PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One, and PC via Steam.

The game fills the gap between *Yakuza 6: The Song of Life* and *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth* — explaining where Kiryu has been and why he's in the emotional state he's in when Infinite Wealth begins. It was positioned and priced as a mid-tier entry rather than a full mainline release.

## The Story

Following the events of Yakuza 6, Kiryu has faked his death and is living under the assumed identity of "Joryu" — working for a shadowy organisation called the Daidoji in exchange for protection for the people he cares about. An assignment at a private resort island called The Castle forces him back into conflict.

## Combat

Gaiden uses the Dragon Engine and features Kiryu's classic fighting styles alongside a new **Agent Style** — a gadget-heavy combat approach that leans into the spy-thriller framing of the Daidoji storyline. Wires, explosives, and deployable objects add variety to a combat system that was already strong.

## Reception

The game was widely praised as a tight, focused entry that did exactly what it needed to do — deliver Kiryu content between mainline games while advancing the story toward Infinite Wealth. At its ~15 hour main story length, it doesn't overstay its welcome.

**If you're planning to play Infinite Wealth, play this first.** The emotional setup it provides makes a real difference.
