## Overview

*Like a Dragon Gaiden* is a shorter, more focused game and its soundtrack reflects that — leaner than the mainline entries, with the musical energy concentrated in the Coliseum sequences and the Agent Style combat. The Sotenbori ambient music revisits established sonic territory from *Yakuza 5* and *Yakuza 0* while updating it for the 2019 setting.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2023 (worldwide simultaneous) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Agent Style battle theme** — The spy-thriller combat aesthetic gets a musical complement: a driving theme with a prominent brass line that leans closer to an action film score than the series' usual rock. It's a noticeable departure that works well for the Joryu persona.

**Grand Coliseum themes** — The escalating crowd ambience system returns here as a full compositional element. Each tier of the Coliseum has its own baseline sound that builds as the fights progress, with the championship bouts having a dedicated theme that feels appropriately climactic for the game's structure.

**Sotenbori night themes** — A 2019 update of the city's established sound. Richer production detail than the Y0/Y5 versions of Sotenbori, with more layered ambient street sound underneath the musical elements.

**Akame Network mission themes** — The substory-style network missions have lighter, more low-key music than the main combat sequences — closer to the investigative sound of Judgment than the mainline Yakuza entries.

---

## Notes on the Score

Several tracks from the Gaiden score transition directly into *Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth* — the two games were developed in parallel and share musical connective tissue around Kiryu's storyline.
