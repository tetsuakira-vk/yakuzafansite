## Overview

*Like a Dragon: Ishin!* posed a unique musical challenge: the Yakuza series' established rock and electronic sound had to coexist with the cultural and historical setting of 1860s Kyoto. The solution was integration rather than substitution — traditional instruments (shamisen, shakuhachi, taiko) are woven into the series' existing musical language rather than replacing it.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Original release:** 2014 (Japan) / Remake: 2023 / Digital streaming available worldwide  
**Note:** The 2023 remake features re-recorded and expanded versions of the original score.

---

## Standout Tracks

**Fushimi exploration themes** — The outdoor Kyoto sequences have a layered ambient sound: shamisen melody over atmospheric synth pads, with temple bells in the distance during specific times of day. It's unlike anything else in the series.

**Swordsman style battle theme** — The primary combat theme for the sword-focused style uses taiko percussion as the rhythmic base, building to a hard rock climax. The blend shouldn't work as well as it does.

**Wild Dancer style theme** — The most kinetic battle music in the game, reflecting the style's mixed-combat chaos. The tempo accelerates over the course of the track as the combo builds.

**Trooper Card battle music** — A deliberately retro arcade-style theme for the card battle system, functioning as a tonal counterpoint to the rest of the score's period drama weight.

**End credits / closing theme** — Uses full traditional ensemble (no electronic elements) for the first time in the main score. A considered choice for the ending of a game that's fundamentally about the death of an era.

---

## Notes on the Score

The original 2014 soundtrack was produced for PS3 audio hardware. The 2023 remake re-recorded the score with expanded instrumentation and higher audio fidelity — the two versions have noticeable differences beyond production quality; some arrangements were revised.
