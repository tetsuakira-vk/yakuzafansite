## Overview

*Yakuza Kiwami 2* marks the full transition to Dragon Engine audio — cleaner production, wider dynamic range, and a score that takes full advantage of not being constrained by PS2- or PS3-era hardware. The Sotenbori sequences in particular have a warmer, more layered sound that distinguishes them from the Kamurocho material.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2017 (Japan) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**"Receive You THE ORIGIN (Kiwami 2 version)"** — A further evolution of the theme, here used more sparingly and with more emotional restraint than in Kiwami 1.

**Majima Construction campaign themes** — The standalone Majima campaign has its own distinct musical palette: scrappier, more chaotic energy to match his street-level operation versus Kiryu's more measured storyline.

**Sotenbori ambient tracks** — The canal district at night has one of the best ambient soundscapes in the series — a mix of reflected neon, faint jazz from the clubs, and the low hum of the city.

**Cabaret Club competition music** — The escalating tension tracks during Grand Prix rounds were redesigned from the ground up. The final round theme has become one of the more memorable pieces in the side-content music catalogue.

---

## Notes on the Score

Kiwami 2 reuses and recontextualises music from the original PS2 *Yakuza 2* score while adding substantial new material for the remade content. The Kaoru Sayama substory (absent from the original Western PS2 release) has its own dedicated theme.
