## Overview

*Yakuza 3* was the first PS3 entry and the score reflects the ambition of the hardware jump. The addition of Okinawa as a second setting gave the composers room to work outside Kamurocho's established sound — the Ryukyu district has its own musical identity built on traditional Okinawan instruments and rhythm structures layered under the series' rock and electronic base.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2010 (Japan, physical CD) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Okinawa ambient and exploration themes** — The beach and morning sequences in particular have a quality unlike anything else in the series. The sound of the Ryukyu district in the early game chapters is genuinely peaceful.

**Morning Glory Orphanage themes** — The children's sequences have dedicated music that leans heavily on acoustic instruments. The contrast when the Kamurocho sections kick in is deliberate and effective.

**"Again" (Kiryu's theme variation)** — A slower, more introspective version of the standard Kiryu battle theme that appears in the Okinawa chapters — reflecting the person he's trying to be there versus who the city demands he be.

**Underground Coliseum music** — The escalating crowd noise integrated into the combat music during Coliseum bouts is a production detail that got more sophisticated in this entry.

---

## Notes on the Score

The 2019 remaster of *Yakuza 3* restored the full soundtrack including music associated with the 17 cut substories and the hostess club and disc golf activities. Western players who only experienced the original PS3 release missed a significant portion of the composed music.
