The Ryu Ga Gotoku series has produced original songs with full music video productions, karaoke performances filmed in-engine, and promotional video content that functions as standalone music video work. Several of these have developed substantial followings entirely outside the game community.

---

## "Baka Mitai" (だめだこりゃ / Dame da ne)

The series' most culturally embedded song. A melancholy enka-pop ballad that first appeared as a karaoke track in *Yakuza 0* and has since appeared in every subsequent mainline entry in some form — usually as a karaoke song performed by the protagonist.

What made it genuinely famous outside gaming was the **deepfake meme** that spread in 2020–2021: the "dame da ne" face-swap videos, in which public figures were placed into Kiryu's karaoke performance. The meme spread widely enough that major news outlets covered it, introducing the song — and the series — to people who had never heard of Yakuza.

The song has been performed at RGG Studio concerts, covered by numerous artists, and released as a standalone digital single. The in-game performance (Kiryu singing alone in a karaoke bar) has become an iconic image of the series.

---

## "For My Life" — Haruka Sawamura (*Yakuza 5*)

Written as part of Haruka's idol career storyline in *Yakuza 5*, this J-pop track was produced to commercial release standard and performed in a full music video sequence within the game. Unlike most in-game music, it was released as a standalone digital single and received genuine radio play in Japan on regional J-pop stations.

The full music video — choreographed and rendered in-engine — is accessible in *Yakuza 5* after completing Haruka's chapter. It functions as a complete music video production, not a cutscene.

---

## "24 Hour Cinderella" (*Yakuza 0*)

The bubble-era disco track from *Yakuza 0*'s karaoke system. Performed by Kiryu in full 80s stage lighting — the in-game music video treatment includes period-appropriate costume and choreography. The track became one of the most memorable pieces of *Yakuza 0*'s soundtrack, partly because of its sheer commitment to the era.

## Promotional Music Videos

Several games have received standalone promotional music video content produced by RGG Studio and SEGA for marketing purposes:

- **"Like a Dragon" opening theme videos** — animated or live-action promotional pieces accompanying major series releases
- **"Infinite Wealth" announcement trailer music** — the Hawaii-set announcement trailer used original music produced specifically for the video before the full OST was released

---

## In-Engine Karaoke Performances

Every game in the series from *Yakuza 0* onwards has produced **in-engine music video sequences** for its karaoke tracks — these are not static minigame screens but fully choreographed, lit, and staged performances rendered in the game's engine. Across the series, this adds up to dozens of unique short-form performance videos. Notable standouts:

- **Majima's karaoke performances** across multiple games — performed with full dramatic commitment regardless of song genre
- **The JUDGEMENT full-cast karaoke sequence** in *Like a Dragon* — Ichiban and party members performing together
- **"Karaoke Battle" sequences** in *Yakuza 0* — competitive karaoke with full audience reaction tracking
