## Overview

*Yakuza 6*'s soundtrack is the most restrained in Kiryu's run of games. The Dragon Engine's improved audio fidelity allowed for quieter, more detailed ambient sound design — and the composers used that space deliberately. The score mirrors the story: Kiryu trying to live a smaller, quieter life, and the city refusing to let him.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2016 (Japan, physical CD) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Onomichi exploration themes** — The coastal town sequences are the most peaceful music in the entire series. Morning themes with acoustic guitar and ambient sea sound. Notably, the Onomichi music has no percussion in the quiet sections — a conscious decision to make the town feel different from every other location in the series.

**Haruto's themes** — The scenes involving the baby have specific, minimal music — mostly piano with long spaces between phrases. It's effective without being sentimental.

**Kamurocho night themes (Dragon Engine)** — The same streets as previous games, but now rendered with layered ambient detail: distant club music, traffic hum, rain on neon. The music that plays here has more texture than any prior version of these streets.

**Battle themes** — A harder, more urgent sound than previous entries. As if the stakes of a final arc are embedded in the percussion patterns.

**"For the Glory" / end credits music** — The closing theme is one of the series' most understated — sparse piano over ambient sound, fading slowly. Intentionally unresolved.

---

## Notes on the Score

The *Song of Life* subtitle is reflected directly in the music — several tracks use vocal elements (wordless choir, hummed melody lines) that hadn't appeared in the series before. It gives certain scenes a different emotional register.
