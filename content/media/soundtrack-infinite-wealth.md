## Overview

*Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth* uses Hawaii's cultural layering as musical raw material. The score incorporates Polynesian rhythmic structures, slack-key guitar, Pacific reggaeton, and the Japanese-American musical crosscurrents that characterise real Honolulu — all filtered through the series' established rock and RPG battle-music language.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** January 2024 (worldwide) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Honolulu exploration themes** — Each district has its own ambient sound. The Chinatown area has Cantonese pop bleed from storefronts; the beach districts have slack-key guitar and surf ambient; the industrial port has a grimier electronic base. The world feels musically coherent in a way that rewards extended exploration.

**Job class battle themes (Hawaii set)** — The Hawaiian job classes (Aquanaut, Cabbie, etc.) have battle themes that integrate Polynesian percussion and brass arrangements. The Aquanaut's underwater combat music is genuinely inventive.

**Kiryu's parallel storyline themes** — Kiryu's scenes have a notably different sonic register from Ichiban's — more restrained, with more space and acoustic instrumentation. The contrast was intentional and tracks with how the two characters process the world differently.

**Boss battle orchestral themes** — The late-game boss battles have the largest orchestral arrangements in the series, reflecting the scale of what's at stake. Full string sections appear in battle music for the first time.

**"New Day" (ending theme)** — A pop song written for the credits. More optimistic in tone than most series closing themes — deliberately so, given where the story ends.
