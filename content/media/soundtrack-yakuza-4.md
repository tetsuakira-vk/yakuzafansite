## Overview

With four playable protagonists, *Yakuza 4*'s composers faced a structural challenge: each character needed a sonic identity that felt distinct without fragmenting the soundtrack into four unrelated scores. The solution was to use a shared harmonic language with different instrumental textures — Akiyama's sections lean jazz, Saejima's lean heavy rock, Tanimura's lean street-level funk, Kiryu's retain the established Dragon Style sound.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2010 (Japan, physical CD) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Akiyama's exploration theme** — The Sky Finance sequences have a smoother, more melodic sound than the rest of the game. Late-night jazz undertones that fit his character well.

**Saejima's prison themes** — The prison chapter has an almost ambient, oppressive sound design. The music is sparse, with industrial percussion elements that create a constant low-level tension.

**Tanimura's Kamurocho theme** — The street-level, improvised feeling of Tanimura's combat style is reflected in a more rhythmically complex battle theme than Kiryu's. Closer to hip-hop percussion structures than the rock base of earlier entries.

**The rooftop sequence music** — The tracks accompanying Tanimura's unique rooftop traversal are some of the best ambient-action pieces in the series.

**Coliseum Champion music** — The four-protagonist Coliseum structure has escalating themes that change depending on which protagonist holds the title. A subtle compositional detail that most players don't consciously register.
