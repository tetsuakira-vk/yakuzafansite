RGG Studio has produced an official art book for most mainline entries, as well as several production-focused volumes covering the broader series. Most are Japan-exclusive releases from publisher KADOKAWA or affiliated imprints. A small number have received official English editions.

---

## Per-Game Art Books

### Yakuza 0
**タイトル:** *龍が如く0 誓いの場所 公式完全攻略本* (Official Complete Guide) and a separate art/design archive  
A two-volume set: the strategy guide contains significant art content (character sheets, environment concepts, enemy design breakdowns), while the design archive focuses on the 1988 aesthetic research — real location photography alongside the in-game interpretations. Highly sought after by series artists and fans interested in the Kabukicho/Dotonbori source material.

### Yakuza Kiwami / Kiwami 2
Both releases have compact official guides with art sections covering the Dragon Engine character model transitions — useful for seeing how the same character designs were rebuilt across engine generations. Kiwami 2's art sections cover Sotenbori's reconstruction in particular detail.

### Yakuza 3 / 4 / 5
PS3-era art books are the most difficult to source outside Japan. The *Yakuza 5* volume is the largest of the three, reflecting the game's scope — five cities with separate environment concept sections for each.

### Yakuza 6: The Song of Life
**タイトル:** *龍が如く6 命の詩。 公式完全攻略本*  
Notable for including production notes on the Dragon Engine's first full deployment and the Onomichi location survey photography. The character design section covers the new secondary cast alongside the Dragon Engine versions of returning characters.

### Yakuza: Like a Dragon
The art book for the RPG reinvention covers the new job class system design — each class has full concept art for all party members, which adds up to substantial material. The Yokohama environment sections document the Isezaki Ijincho location research.

### Like a Dragon: Ishin!
Both the 2014 original and the 2023 remake have art books. The remake edition includes a comparative section showing how assets were rebuilt between the PS3/PS4 version and the Dragon Engine version — a genuine production document for anyone interested in asset pipeline work.

### Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth
The most recent major art book. Hawaii location photography, job class design across the expanded roster, and the most detailed character model sheet section the series has produced for a single volume.

## Series-Wide Volumes

***Ryu Ga Gotoku Series Art Works*** — A compilation volume covering the full mainline series up to *Yakuza 6*, published to coincide with the series' 15th anniversary. Character design evolution from PS2 through Dragon Engine is particularly well presented. Japan only.

**Dragon Engine Technical Design Book** — A production-focused volume covering the engine's visual and audio systems. More technical than typical art books; aimed at developers and technical artists as much as fans. Extremely limited print run; now commands high prices on Japanese secondhand markets (Mercari Japan, Yahoo Auctions JP).

## Sourcing

Most art books are Japan-exclusive and out of print. Reliable sourcing options:
- **CDJapan** (cdj.com) — stocks new releases and some back catalogue
- **Mercari Japan** — best for out-of-print volumes; use a proxy service if outside Japan
- **Amazon Japan** — new and secondhand listings
- **Mandarake** (mandarake.co.jp) — specialist secondhand Japanese media; reliable grading

Expect to pay ¥2,500–¥5,000 for standard guides, ¥8,000–¥20,000+ for the rarer design archive volumes in good condition.
