## Overview

Majima as a pirate captain demanded a different musical register than anything the series had done before. The *Pirate Yakuza in Hawaii* score leans into nautical drama without irony: sea shanty structures, fife-and-drum arrangements, cannon-shot percussion, all driven through the series' high-energy action music engine. It's theatrical and it knows it.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** February 2025 (worldwide) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Majima's pirate theme** — A swaggering nautical march built around a repeating fife motif. Captures the comedy and menace simultaneously, which is the only way a Majima theme can work.

**Ship combat music** — The naval battle sequences have a full orchestral sound with brass-heavy arrangements and tympani percussion. Closest to a film score the series has produced for an action sequence.

**Port town exploration themes** — Each port has its own ambient character. The main Hawaii port blends the established Infinite Wealth Pacific sound with a rougher, saltier texture appropriate to pirate territory.

**Crew recruitment themes** — Light, comedic arrangements that play during recruitment conversations. Several feature brief musical characterisations of the individual crew members — a running joke embedded in the score.

**Final battle themes** — Full orchestral with choir elements. The operatic scale suits where the story goes in the finale.
