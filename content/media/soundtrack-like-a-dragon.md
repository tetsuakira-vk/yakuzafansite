## Overview

The shift to turn-based RPG mechanics required a fundamental rethinking of the music system. *Yakuza: Like a Dragon*'s score replaces the loop-heavy action battle tracks with structured musical cues built around the rhythm of turn-based combat — tension on enemy turns, escalation on party builds, resolution on decisive attacks.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2020 (Japan, physical CD) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Job class battle themes** — Each job class has a distinct battle theme variation. The Hero class theme has a classic JRPG brass fanfare that is unmistakably a Dragon Quest homage. The Breakdancer battle music is the furthest stylistic departure in the soundtrack — genuine hip-hop production.

**Isezaki Ijincho exploration themes** — Yokohama's districts have individual ambient soundscapes. Kotown has the most distinctive — a mix of Korean pop influences and street-level bass that distinguishes it from the Japanese entertainment district music elsewhere.

**Party banter music** — The substory and party conversation scenes use a dedicated ensemble sound (small jazz combo) that plays throughout the side content, giving it a different texture from the main story scenes.

**"Receive You" (Ichiban version)** — A rearrangement of the series' anchor theme recontextualised for a different protagonist and a different story relationship. Appears at a specific late-game moment.

**Boss battle escalation tracks** — Turn-based boss fights require musical pacing built around phases rather than continuous action. The later-game boss themes have dramatic mid-fight key changes that land harder than they have any right to.
