## Overview

The *Yakuza 0* original soundtrack is the defining musical statement of the series. Released as a three-disc CD set in Japan in 2015, it captures the excess of the bubble economy era through a mix of jazz-funk, city pop, driving synth-rock, and the late-80s electronic sound that saturated Japanese entertainment districts.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji, Hosoe Shin'ichi, Yoko Sato, Akiko Hasegawa  
**Release:** 2015 (Japan, 3-disc physical) / Digital streaming available worldwide  
**Tracks:** 99

---

## Standout Tracks

**"Pledge of Demon"** — Kiryu's primary battle theme. A hard-driving rock track that became the sound most associated with Dragon Style combat. Reprised and remastered across subsequent entries.

**"Friday Night"** — Majima's Sotenbori theme. Laid-back and slightly melancholy under the swagger — it captures his situation in the story better than the script does.

**"Funk Goes on"** — The standard street battle theme. One of the most recognisable pieces in the entire series; the funk baseline has been in players' heads since launch.

**"Machine Gun Kiss"** — Majima's combat theme, frantic and unhinged in the best way.

**"24 Hour Cinderella"** — The iconic karaoke track. Originally performed as a full J-pop song with a music video within the game; achieved genuine popularity outside the game.

**"Receive You ~Reborn~"** — A late-game battle arrangement with additional emotional weight from where in the story it appears.

---

## Physical Release

The Japan CD release includes liner notes with composer commentary and full track credits. Packaging reflected the game's 80s aesthetic — gold-foil text on black, reminiscent of high-end Japanese vinyl sleeves of the period.

A vinyl edition was later produced in limited quantities and has become a collector's item.
