## Overview

*Yakuza Kiwami*'s soundtrack does double duty: it preserves and remaster the original *Yakuza* (2005) compositions for modern hardware, while adding entirely new music written for content that didn't exist in the original — primarily Majima Everywhere's extended encounter system and the Nishikiyama flashback chapters.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji, plus original arrangements from the 2005 Yakuza score  
**Release:** 2016 (Japan) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**"Receive You"** — The original version of what became one of the series' most recognisable themes. Kiwami's remaster adds production depth while keeping the emotional core intact.

**"Receive You THE ORIGIN"** — A stripped-back acoustic rearrangement used in specific emotional scenes in Kiwami. Unexpectedly affecting.

**Majima Everywhere encounter themes** — Each of Majima's disguise forms has its own short musical signature that plays on detection. The Officer Majima theme in particular has a darkly comedic brass arrangement.

**"Baka Mitai" (Dame da ne)** — Featured in the karaoke sections. The *Yakuza 0* version introduced it; Kiwami's arrangement is a slightly different mix suited to the Kiryu-only narrative.

---

## Notes on the Score

The original 2005 Yakuza score was composed for PS2 hardware constraints. Kiwami's remaster retained the compositional structure of key themes but re-recorded with expanded instrumentation. Several tracks that were effectively cut in the original Western PS2 release — due to the 17 removed substories — are present and complete in Kiwami.
