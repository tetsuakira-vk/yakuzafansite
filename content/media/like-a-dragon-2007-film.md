## Synopsis

*Like a Dragon* adapts the story of the original *Yakuza* (PS2, 2005) — Kazuma Kiryu walks out of prison after ten years, finds Kamurocho transformed, ten billion yen missing, and a young girl named Haruka at the centre of it all.

Director **Takashi Miike** was the natural choice for the material. Already known internationally for *Audition* and *Ichi the Killer*, Miike had spent his career working in precisely the idiom the Yakuza games occupied: yakuza procedurals, extreme violence played for operatic effect, and an underlying deadpan absurdism. He understood the assignment.

---

## Cast

**Kazuki Kitamura** brings a quieter, more internalised Kiryu than the game's version — less Dragon of Dojima, more man exhausted by the world he can't escape. It's a grounded interpretation.

**Goro Kishitani** as Majima is the film's standout. He brings the wild-eyed unpredictability of the game version while grounding it in something more genuinely unhinged. His scenes are the best in the film.

---

## Reception

*Like a Dragon* received mixed reviews on release but has developed a following among fans of both Miike's work and the game series. It functions better as a companion piece than a standalone film — its most resonant moments are those that feel like the games finding their cinematic equivalent.
