The Ryu Ga Gotoku franchise has an extensive parallel life in Japan's pachinko and pachislot (gambling machine) industry. These releases are significant enough to have their own dedicated marketing campaigns, original animated sequences, and in some cases new voice recordings from the main cast. They represent a substantial segment of the franchise's Japanese commercial activity that is almost entirely invisible to Western audiences.

---

## What Is Pachinko / Pachislot?

**Pachinko** is a Japanese amusement machine combining elements of pinball and slot mechanics. **Pachislot** machines are similar but use a reel-based slot system. Both are played in dedicated pachinko parlours across Japan — a massive industry. Major entertainment franchises (film, anime, video games) frequently license their properties for pachinko machines, which are produced by specialist manufacturers.

The machines are not games in the traditional sense — they use the franchise's characters, music, and visual identity as the setting for the gambling mechanic, with animated cutscenes playing during significant moments.

---

## Yakuza / Ryu Ga Gotoku Pachinko Machines

Multiple pachinko machines based on the Yakuza series have been produced and released in Japan. Manufacturers have included **Daiichi Shokai** and **Sankyo**, among others.

**What these machines typically include:**
- Animated cutscenes using the game's assets (sometimes with new animation not seen in the games)
- New voice recordings from the main cast (Kazuhiro Yamaji as Kiryu, Hidenari Ugaki as Majima, etc.)
- Music from the game soundtracks integrated into the machine's audio
- Dramatic recreations of key story moments from the games

**Notable releases:**
- *CRぱちんこ 龍が如く* — Based on the mainline series, primarily drawing from early Kiryu entries
- Machines based on *Yakuza 0* and the Kiwami remakes, which were produced during the series' renewed popularity surge in the mid-2010s

## Why These Matter for Fans

The pachinko machines have produced some content that exists nowhere else:

- **Original animated sequences** — some machines commissioned short animated scenes depicting Kamurocho that aren't available in the games or any other format
- **New voice performances** — cast members recorded lines specifically for the machines, some of which expand on character moments from the games
- **Promotional material** — flyers, display art, and promotional videos for pachinko releases sometimes feature character art commissioned separately from the game's official materials

---

## Arcade Releases

Beyond pachinko, the franchise has had a small number of arcade-specific releases in Japan:

- **Rhythm game crossovers** — Yakuza characters and music have appeared in major Japanese arcade rhythm games including *maimai* and *CHUNITHM*, bringing the series' karaoke catalogue into the rhythm game format
- **Crane game prizes** — Yakuza-themed prize figures for crane (UFO catcher) machines have been a steady source of accessible merchandise in Japanese arcades since the mid-2000s. These are distinct from the premium figure releases and represent more accessible price points

---

## Accessing Pachinko Content

Pachinko machines are Japan-only and not legally exportable for use as gambling devices. However:

- **YouTube** has significant amounts of pachinko gameplay footage, including the cutscene sequences
- **Nicovideo** has historical footage from older machine releases
- Fan communities have archived much of the animated content

Search terms: "龍が如く パチンコ" (Ryu Ga Gotoku pachinko) for Japanese-language sources with the most complete coverage.
