## Overview

*Judgment* deliberately distances itself musically from the mainline Yakuza series. Where Kiryu's games use rock and electronic battle music rooted in the series' established language, Yagami's score pulls from noir cinema, legal drama, and investigation thriller — it's cooler, more methodical, suited to a protagonist who solves problems with evidence and fists rather than just fists.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2018 (Japan) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Yagami's investigation theme** — A slow-building piano and bass combination that plays during deduction sequences. Deliberately paced to match the investigative process — builds tension without resolution until the evidence chain closes.

**Crane Style battle theme** — Fast, angular, rhythmically complex. Matches the style's fluid, evasive combat philosophy.

**Tiger Style battle theme** — Heavier, more aggressive. A harder rock base that pushes harder than Crane's more melodic approach.

**Courtroom sequences** — Tense orchestral music for the trial sections. Draws on the drama of Japanese courtroom procedure — the silences are used as effectively as the music itself.

**Kamurocho ambient (Judgment version)** — The same city Kiryu has walked, but Yagami's version sounds different — slightly colder, more observational. The producers specifically noted they wanted the city to feel like somewhere Yagami is working rather than somewhere he belongs.
