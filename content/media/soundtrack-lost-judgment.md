## Overview

*Lost Judgment* opens the soundtrack to a second city and the broader social canvas of the school stories. The score handles both the tense legal-thriller main narrative and the lighter, more human-scale school club side content — and manages the tonal range better than any prior Yakuza entry with mixed-weight side content.

**Composers:** Hidenori Shoji and the RGG Studio sound team  
**Release:** 2021 (Japan) / Digital streaming available worldwide

---

## Standout Tracks

**Skateboard traversal themes** — The most distinctive new music in the game. Free-moving traversal through Yokohama needed something faster and lighter than the standard exploration ambient — what the composers produced is closer to a lo-fi hip-hop/street skate aesthetic, which lands exactly right for how the movement feels.

**Seiryo High School themes** — The school sequences have dedicated lighter music for the hallway and classroom scenes — adolescent and slightly awkward in tone, which is appropriate. The contrast when combat music breaks through is effectively jarring.

**Social Club themes** — Each club has a short musical motif that plays in its space. The dance club theme, boxing gym theme, and motorbike club themes are individually distinct — the production team clearly enjoyed giving each its own small identity.

**Yokohama (Isezaki Ijincho) ambient** — Revisiting the same city as *Yakuza: Like a Dragon* from Yagami's perspective. The music uses the same streets but with the colder, more investigative colouring from Yagami's established sound. Comparing the two ambient tracks for the same location is an interesting exercise.

**Kaito Files DLC themes** — The standalone Kaito campaign has its own sound — rougher and more physical than Yagami's score, reflecting Kaito's direct approach to everything.
